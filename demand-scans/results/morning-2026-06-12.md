## General Trends

1. **AI + Human collaboration is the premium model.** Both Upwork and Fiverr data show the same pattern: AI handles generation/volume, humans handle editing/refinement/judgment. Freelancers who offer "AI-powered [service] with human quality control" command higher rates. Theo's automation + Kyle's review is literally this model.

2. **Small businesses want execution, not advice.** Fiverr's Shopify data is clear — generic "Shopify expert" searches grew +8%, but "Shopify development" (+330%) and "Shopify graphics" (+348%) exploded. The market is tired of consultants; they want people who deliver tangible output. Theo should frame services as deliverables, not advice.

3. **Data cleaning is a sleeper hit.** Excel data cleaning (+210%), PDF-to-Excel (+153%), and data entry (+10% baseline) collectively show that messy data is a universal pain point. AI makes data processing faster but humans still need to validate. Theo could build a "send me your spreadsheet, get back a cleaned, formatted version" service that runs almost entirely on automation.
