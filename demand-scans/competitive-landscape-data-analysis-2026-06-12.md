## The Market: Three Tiers

### Tier 1: Big analytics agencies (£3k-10k+/mo)
**Examples:** Tydo ($4,500/mo+), Vision Labs, Darkroom ($8k-25k/mo), Improvado ($5k+/mo)

| What they offer | What they cost | Who they serve |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|
| Full-service analytics + marketing + creative | $4,500-25,000/mo | Medium-to-large businesses, serious ecommerce brands |
| Custom dashboards, data warehousing, attribution | Single project: $15,000-50,000 | Clients who already spend £50k+/mo on ads |
| Dedicated account manager + team | Annual contracts | Not small businesses |

**Our advantage:** They're unaffordable for small businesses. They also over-deliver — most small shops don't need a data warehouse, they need someone to read their Shopify dashboard and tell them what's wrong.

### Tier 2: AI-powered freelancers / boutique consultants (£1,500-4,000/mo)
**Examples:** The "AI freelancer playbook" crowd, niche data consultants

| What they offer | What they cost | Who they serve |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|
| Recurring data analysis + reporting | $1,500-4,000/mo per client | Ecommerce stores, service businesses |
| AI-leveraged delivery (faster, cheaper) | 60% profit margins | Clients who grew out of DIY but can't afford Tier 1 |
| Monthly deliverable (report, dashboard, insights) | Usually 3-6 month contracts | Growing businesses |

**This is our natural lane.** The article that surfaced this (Anuj's "AI Freelancer's Playbook") confirms someone is already doing exactly this model at $1,500-4,000/mo with 60% margins. We can enter at a lower price point to build case studies, then increase.

### Tier 3: Solo freelancers on Upwork/Fiverr ($25-50/h, $500-2,000/project)
**Examples:** The 376+ data analysts on Upwork at any given time

| What they offer | What they cost | Who they serve |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|
| One-off data cleaning, Excel sheets, GA setup | $25-50/h median | One-off projects, budget-constrained |
| Single dashboards, survey analysis | $120-500 fixed price | Smallest businesses, startups |
| Variable quality, slow turnaround | Fiverr takes 20% cut | Clients who don't know what they need |

**Our advantage:** We automate the processing (Theo), add better narrative (Claude), and review with human judgment (Kyle). Our turnaround is faster and our output quality is higher — for the same or slightly higher price.

## Who's Posting Jobs (the niches)

Extracted from Upwork's 376 active data analyst listings:

### 1. E-commerce / Retail (largest cluster)
- Shopify analytics, sales data, product performance
- Customer behaviour, churn analysis, cohort tracking
- Sample: "Data Analyst for Ecommerce Data — Google Analytics, Shopify, Recharge, Google Sheets"
- **Demand:** Very high. Lots of data, owners too busy to analyse it.

### 2. Real Estate (well-paying niche)
- Vacant land pricing, property valuation, market analysis
- Sample: "Pricing Analyst for Vacant Land — Market Research, Real Estate Appraisal, Financial Modeling"
- **Demand:** Medium-high. Niche-specific knowledge needed but pays well.

### 3. Healthcare (regulated, steady)
- Medicaid data, T-MSIS/CMS access, clinic analytics
- Sample: "Medicaid Data Expert — requires existing T-MSIS/CMS access"
- **Demand:** Medium. Higher barrier (compliance), but sticky clients.

### 4. Local Government / Education (bureaucratic, stable)
- Impact analysis, academic research, program evaluation
- Sample: "Learning and Impact Analyst — Market Analysis, Academic Writing, Statistics"
- **Demand:** Lower volume but stable, long-term projects.

### 5. Small Service Businesses (most underserved)
- Spreadsheet-based, no existing analytics
- Don't even know what data analysis looks like
- High volume of "data entry" and "admin" jobs that are really analysis in disguise
- **Demand:** Very high but fragmented. Hardest to reach.

### 6. Marketing Agencies (outsourcing partners)
- Hire freelancers to do the analysis they promised their clients
- Sample: "Research & Data Analyst — Market Research, document review into Google Sheets"
- **Demand:** High. Recurring if you deliver consistently.

## What Existing Providers Actually Deliver

### Common offerings (across all tiers)

| Service | Type | Typical price | Recurring? |
|---------|------|---------------|------------|
| Data cleaning | One-off | $30-200 | No |
| Excel spreadsheet creation | One-off | $50-500 | Rarely |
| Google Analytics setup/audit | One-off | $200-1,000 | No |
| Custom dashboard (Power BI/Tableau) | One-off | $500-5,000 | Updates extra |
| Survey analysis | One-off | $200-2,000 | No |
| Recurring monthly report | Recurring | $500-4,000/mo | Yes |
| Full analytics consulting | Project | $5,000-50,000 | Usually project |
| Data pipeline/API build | Project | $1,000-20,000 | Maintenance extra |

### What's missing

**No one is offering a "small business health report" as a standalone, affordable product.**

- Agencies bundle it into £5k+ retainers (too expensive)
- Freelancers offer one-off cleaning or dashboards (no recurring narrative)
- AI tools generate generic output (no real data processing)

The gap: a recurring, affordable, insightful report that costs less than an agency but delivers more than a freelancer — and requires zero setup, training, or site visits.

## The Ecommerce Sweet Spot

Ecommerce is the best entry niche because:

1. **Data is abundant** — Shopify exports sales, customers, products, traffic. Every store generates it.
2. **Owners are time-poor** — Running a store is full-time. They don't have time to analyse.
3. **They already spend money** — They have ad budgets ($500-10k/mo), inventory costs, tools subscriptions. They understand the value of data.
4. **Low barriers** — Shopify CSV exports are standard. No API setup needed. Theo can process a Shopify export right now.
5. **Repeatable** — Month 2's report is easier than Month 1's because we have baseline data.

### What we'd tell an ecommerce store:
> "You have Shopify analytics. You have Google Analytics. You might even have Triple Whale. But nobody is reading all of them together and telling you what's actually happening in your business once a month. That's what we do. Send us your exports, we send back a report with 3-5 things you can fix."

## The Small Business Underserved Gap

The strongest argument for going after micro/small businesses:

- **Agencies ignore them** — too small for the £5k/mo minimum
- **Freelancers don't retain them** — one-off projects don't build relationships
- **They're overwhelmed** — The Reddit data shows "drowning in admin" is the #1 pain point across r/smallbusiness, r/selfemployed, r/freelance
- **They'd value a £97-197 report** — It's less than one day of their own time, and saves them hours of spreadsheet wrestling
- **They're loyal** — A good relationship with a small business owner can last years

The counterpoint: small businesses are harder to reach (no marketing budget, don't search for "data analyst"). But a single case study with a real result is worth more than £1,000 of ads.
