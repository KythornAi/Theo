## 1. STRATEGIC FOUNDATION

### 1.1 Purpose of Pinterest for Paper N Pixels

Pinterest is the **Month-1 traffic channel**. Primary goals:
- Drive traffic to Etsy listings
- Drive traffic to blog posts
- Build early awareness before launch
- Validate pain-point messaging before products are live
- Collect data on which ADHD productivity angles get saves, clicks, and outbound traffic

### 1.2 Brand Positioning

**Paper N Pixels helps adults with ADHD reset their day without starting over.**

Core differentiation:
- Browser-based ADHD planning tool, not another printable PDF
- Designed for real ADHD behavior: overwhelm, avoidance, low energy, initiation friction, planner abandonment
- Focuses on "what can I do next?" rather than perfect planning

Competitor contrast:
- FutureADHD, Daily Focus Club, Manifestable: mostly PDF, GoodNotes, printable systems
- PNP: digital, browser-based, flexible, forgiving, reset-friendly

### 1.3 Target Audience

Adults with ADHD, primarily women ages 25-45.

Core struggles:
- Abandoning planners
- Feeling behind by 10 a.m.
- Avoiding tasks because starting feels too big
- Buying productivity tools, then not using them
- Having energy that changes throughout the day
- Needing structure but resisting rigid systems

Emotional state while scrolling:
- Avoidant
- Overwhelmed
- Searching for relief
- Tired of being told to "just plan better"
- Drawn to pins that feel personally accurate

### 1.4 Core Messaging Principle

**The pin itself must prove that PNP understands ADHD.**

A strong PNP pin should make the viewer think:
> "That is exactly what happens to me."

Before it says:
> "Buy this planner."

## 3. PIN TEMPLATE LIBRARY

### 3.1 Pain Point Recognition Pin
**Best for:** Top-of-funnel saves and emotional resonance.

Format:
- Large headline (4-9 words)
- One relatable ADHD pain point
- Soft visual background or mockup
- CTA to blog, product, or freebie

Examples:
- "You don't need a new planner. You need a reset button."
- "If your planner only works on good brain days, it is not built for ADHD."
- "When your day is already ruined by 10 a.m."

### 3.2 Problem/Solution Pin
**Best for:** Product and Etsy pins.

Format:
- Top: problem
- Middle: product visual or mockup
- Bottom: solution/CTA

Examples:
- "Can't start your to-do list?" → "Try a 3-task ADHD reset instead."
- "Daily Reset helps you pick the next doable step."

### 3.3 "Not Another Planner" Pin
**Best for:** Differentiating PNP from PDFs and GoodNotes products.

Format:
- Bold headline
- Browser/app screenshot or mockup
- Clear product positioning

Examples:
- "Not another printable you'll forget exists."
- "An ADHD planner that lives in your browser."
- "For the planner-abandonment phase of ADHD productivity."

**Key rule:** Do not attack printables directly. Position PNP as an alternative for people who already know PDFs don't work for them.

### 3.4 Mini Tutorial Pin
**Best for:** Blog traffic and saves.

Format:
- 3 to 5 steps
- Clear title
- Designed for easy saving

Examples:
- "How to reset an ADHD day in 5 minutes"
- "The 3-task rule for overwhelming days"
- "How to brain dump without creating a second to-do list"

CTA: "Read the full guide" / "Get the free reset page" / "Try the Daily Reset method"

### 3.5 Checklist Pin
**Best for:** Saves.

Format:
- Checklist layout
- Short, scannable items
- ADHD-specific title

Examples:
- "Low-Energy Day Checklist"
- "Before You Rewrite Your Whole To-Do List"
- "ADHD Task Initiation Checklist"

Checklist items should be concrete:
- Drink water
- Pick one warm-up task
- Set a 10-minute timer
- Choose 3 tasks only
- Hide the rest

**Avoid vague items:** "Be productive" / "Stay focused" / "Get motivated"

### 3.6 Comparison Pin
**Best for:** Product differentiation.

Format:
- Two columns
- "Traditional planner" vs "ADHD reset tool"
- Keep tone compassionate, not smug

| Traditional Planner | Daily Reset |
|---------------------|-------------|
| Plans the whole day | Starts from now |
| Assumes stable energy | Has low-energy mode |
| Gets abandoned when you fall behind | Helps choose the next doable task |

### 3.7 Quote/Truth Pin
**Best for:** Awareness and saves.

Format:
- Large text
- Minimal visual clutter
- Strong emotional line

Examples:
- "ADHD productivity starts with reducing friction, not increasing pressure."
- "You are not bad at planning. Your planner might be bad at recovery."
- "Initiation is the task."

**Use sparingly:** Good for saves, but may produce weaker outbound clicks unless paired with a blog or freebie.

### 3.8 Product Mockup Pin
**Best for:** Etsy/Gumroad conversion.

Format:
- Multiple product screenshots
- Clear use case
- Keyword-rich headline

Examples:
- "ADHD Daily Planner for Overwhelming Days"
- "Brain Dump Template for ADHD Adults"
- "Low-Energy Digital Planner"

Must include: Product name, use case, visual proof, destination CTA.

### 3.9 "Save This For Later" Pin
**Best for:** Saves and retargetable interest.

Format:
- Practical mini-framework
- Explicit save-worthy value

Examples:
- "Save this for your next stuck day."
- "Save this ADHD reset when your to-do list feels impossible."
- "A 5-minute reset for executive dysfunction days."

### 3.10 Seasonal/Contextual Pin
**Best for:** Trend waves.

Seasonal angles:
- New year planning
- Back to school
- Spring reset
- Sunday reset
- Monday overwhelm
- Holiday recovery
- Q4 burnout
- End-of-month reset

Examples:
- "ADHD Sunday Reset That Does Not Require a Perfect Week"
- "New Year Planning for People Who Abandon Planners"
- "Back-to-Routine ADHD Reset"

**Pro tip from research:** Pin seasonal content 2-3 months early. Pinterest's TransActV2 algorithm (June 2025) now tracks 16,000 lifetime actions per user and recognizes seasonal patterns. If someone searched "spring garden ideas" last March, Pinterest will surface your spring content to them this March.

### 4.2 Keyword Placement

Every pin should include keywords in:
- **File name** (e.g., `adhd-daily-reset-browser-planner-low-energy-day.png`)
- **Pin title** (front-load the main keyword)
- **Pin description** (1-3 keyword phrases woven naturally)
- **Board name** (specific and keyword-rich)
- **Board description** (2-4 sentences with primary and secondary keywords)
- **Linked page** (ensure landing page copy reinforces the same terms)
- **Image text** (when natural)

**Example file name:** `adhd-daily-reset-browser-planner-low-energy-day.png`

**Example title:** `ADHD Daily Reset Planner for Low-Energy Days`

**Example description:** `A gentle ADHD daily planning tool for adults who struggle with planner abandonment, executive dysfunction, and overwhelming to-do lists. Use Daily Reset to choose three doable tasks and restart your day from where you are.`

### 4.3 SEO Rules for Scribe

1. Use one primary keyword per pin
2. Use two to four secondary keywords naturally
3. **Do not keyword-stuff**
4. **Do not write generic descriptions** like "Check out this amazing planner"
5. Make descriptions personal and specific
6. Match pin text, title, and destination — Pinterest rewards consistency
7. Name files before uploading
8. Use board names that contain searchable terms
9. **Board context is now a direct ranking factor** — save new Pins first to the most relevant, keyword-optimized board
10. Build topical authority by sticking to a coherent niche (don't mix unrelated topics)

### 4.4 Search Intent Buckets

Create pins for four intent levels:

**Awareness:**
- "Why planners fail for ADHD brains"
- "ADHD productivity starts with initiation"

**Problem-aware:**
- "Can't start your to-do list?"
- "Overwhelmed by your brain dump?"

**Solution-aware:**
- "Try the 3-task rule"
- "Use a low-energy day plan"

**Buyer-aware:**
- "ADHD daily planner for adults"
- "Browser-based ADHD planner"

### 4.5 Pinterest Algorithm Priorities (2025-2026)

Based on current algorithm analysis:

**What the algorithm rewards:**
- **Fresh content** — new images/videos prioritized in distribution
- **Saves** — the strongest signal that a Pin is valuable
- **Outbound clicks** — traffic quality matters
- **Video watch time** — video pins favored for reach
- **Domain quality** — users click through and stay = good; high bounce = bad
- **Pinner quality** — posting consistency + engagement
- **Topic relevance** — board context is a direct ranking factor
- **Entity recognition** — accounts that consistently cover a specific topic gain authority

**What the algorithm penalizes:**
- Posting the exact same Pin repeatedly (create 3-5 variations instead)
- Low-quality engagement (high bounce rates from your domain)
- Misleading creatives (clickbait headlines, mismatched landing pages)
- Vague/misc boards (use precise, optimized boards)
- Inactivity / inconsistent posting
- Non-original content (repinning less effective than fresh content)

**Optimal posting frequency:** 2-5 fresh Pins per day. Quality over volume. Consistency matters more than bursts.

**Pin lifespan:** Average Pin drives traffic for 3.76 months. Focus on evergreen content.

## 5. PIN CREATION WORKFLOW

### 5.1 Weekly Planning

Every week, choose:
- 2 product angles
- 2 blog angles
- 1 lead magnet angle
- 1 differentiation angle
- 1 seasonal or trend angle

**Output target:** 15-30 fresh pins per week. Multiple pins can point to the same URL if the creative and hook are meaningfully different.

### 5.2 Research Step

For each batch:
1. Choose the destination URL or future destination
2. Identify the primary keyword
3. Search Pinterest manually or with PinClicks
4. Record related keywords
5. Identify top pin patterns: headlines, colors, layouts, repeated phrases, visual formats
6. Note what is missing from competitors

**Specifically look for:**
- Pins that overpromise
- Pins that feel too neurotypical
- Pins that assume high energy
- Pins that rely on pretty planner aesthetics but ignore initiation

### 5.3 Hook Generation

For each destination, generate 10-20 hooks across categories:
- **Pain point:** "Your planner should not fall apart when your day does."
- **Contrarian:** "Stop planning your whole day when you can't start one task."
- **Specific method:** "The 3-task reset for ADHD overwhelm."
- **Emotional recognition:** "For the day you already feel behind."
- **Product promise:** "A browser-based planner for ADHD reset days."
- **Curiosity:** "The planning rule that works better than a full to-do list."

Then choose: 3 save-focused hooks, 3 click-focused hooks, 3 buyer-intent hooks.

### 5.4 Design Step

For each pin:
- Use **1000x1500px** as default
- Use **1000x2100px** for tutorial, checklist, or multi-step pins
- Use large readable text
- Keep headline to 4-9 words
- Use multiple screenshots or product mockups when relevant
- Use real product screenshots once available
- **Avoid AI-generated lifestyle imagery** (Pinterest is cracking down)
- Use cohesive PNP colors, but vary backgrounds enough that the feed doesn't look repetitive

**Design must pass the "ADHD scroll test":**
- Can the main idea be understood in under 2 seconds?
- Does the headline name a real ADHD pain?
- Is there one clear visual focus?
- Is the text readable on mobile?
- Does it feel useful, not decorative?

### 5.5 Copy Step

For each pin, create:
- File name (keyword-rich)
- Pin title
- Pin description
- Destination URL
- Board assignment
- CTA
- Keyword notes
- Hypothesis being tested

### 5.6 Publishing Step

Before publishing, confirm:
- File name is keyword-rich
- Title matches visual hook
- Description includes primary keyword naturally
- Destination URL is correct
- Board is relevant
- No text is too small
- No claim is too broad or medicalized
- Pin does not look like generic wellness content

### 5.7 Linking to Etsy

For static, carousel, and video pins:
1. Destination link = your Etsy listing URL (or Etsy Share & Save link to reduce fees)
2. Ensure the image matches the listing (Pinterest's spam detection compares imagery + URL)
3. Title and description contain the same main keyword phrases as the listing
4. Claim your Etsy shop on Pinterest Business account for domain verification

### 5.8 Tracking Step

After publishing, log the pin in Google Sheets:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Date published | When it went live |
| Pin URL | Direct link to the pin |
| Destination URL | Etsy listing or blog post |
| Board | Which board it was saved to |
| Category | Product / blog / lead magnet |
| Pin format | Static / video / carousel / Idea Pin |
| Hook | The headline used |
| Primary keyword | Main target keyword |
| Secondary keywords | Additional keywords |
| Design template | Which template was used |
| CTA | Call to action |
| Impressions | From Pinterest Analytics |
| Saves | From Pinterest Analytics |
| Pin clicks | From Pinterest Analytics |
| Outbound clicks | From Pinterest Analytics |
| CTR | Click-through rate |
| Save rate | Saves / impressions |
| Notes | Observations |
| Next action | What to do next |

## 6. METRICS & ITERATION

### 6.1 Core Metrics

Track:
- **Impressions** — Pinterest distribution
- **Saves** — usefulness and resonance (strongest value signal)
- **Pin clicks** — interest in the pin
- **Outbound clicks** — traffic quality
- **Outbound CTR** — conversion from Pinterest to site/Etsy
- **Save rate** — saves / impressions
- **Etsy visits** — from Pinterest referral
- **Etsy favorites** — from Pinterest referral
- **Email signups** — from lead magnet pins
- **Sales** — once live

### 6.2 Interpretation Guide

| Pattern | Meaning | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| High impressions, low clicks | Hook broad but not compelling; CTA weak; design pretty but not urgent | Need clearer problem/solution |
| Low impressions, high save rate | Good content, weak SEO or board match | Rework title, description, keywords; create more variations |
| High saves, low outbound clicks | Pin is useful but self-contained | Add stronger reason to click ("get the full reset" / "download the free page") |
| High outbound clicks, low conversion | Destination mismatch | Check Etsy listing first image; ensure blog intro matches pin promise |
| Low everything | Hook too generic; design not readable; keyword too competitive; wrong board; not specific enough to ADHD | Full redesign needed |

### 6.3 Iteration Cadence

**Weekly:**
- Review pins from 7-14 days ago for early signals
- Identify top hooks by save rate
- Identify top keywords by impressions
- Identify top formats by outbound CTR

**Monthly:**
- Create 5-10 new variations from the best performers
- Retire weak templates
- Update board strategy
- Compare product vs blog vs lead magnet traffic
- Identify new content opportunities from search terms

## 7. A/B TESTING SYSTEM

### 7.1 What to Test

Only test one major variable at a time:
- Hook
- Pin format
- Color palette
- Product screenshot vs lifestyle mockup
- Pain-point headline vs solution headline
- Short pin vs long pin
- "ADHD planner" vs "Daily Reset"
- "Browser-based" vs "digital"
- "Executive dysfunction" vs simpler language like "can't start"

### 7.2 Test Structure

For each test:
- Same destination URL
- Same board or closely related board
- Same keyword family
- 2 to 4 creative variants
- Published within the same week
- Tracked for at least 14-30 days

### 7.3 Example A/B Tests

**Test 1: Pain point vs product promise**
- A: "Your planner should not fall apart when your day does."
- B: "A browser-based ADHD planner for reset days."

**Test 2: Method vs emotion**
- A: "The 3-task rule for ADHD overwhelm."
- B: "For the day you already feel behind."

**Test 3: Differentiation**
- A: "Not another printable you'll forget exists."
- B: "An ADHD planner that lives in your browser."

**Test 4: CTA**
- A: "Read the reset method."
- B: "Get the free ADHD reset page."

### 7.4 Winning Criteria

| Goal | Primary Metric |
|------|---------------|
| Awareness | Impressions, saves, save rate |
| Traffic | Outbound clicks, outbound CTR |
| Product | Etsy visits, favorites, add to cart, sales |
| Lead magnets | Email signup rate |

## 9. COMMON MISTAKES

### 9.1 Sounding Too Neurotypical

**Avoid:**
- "Just organize your day"
- "Plan your perfect morning"
- "Wake up at 5 a.m. and crush your goals"
- "Stay consistent every day"

**Better:**
- "Start from where you are"
- "Pick the next doable task"
- "Reset your day without rewriting everything"

### 9.2 Selling Aesthetic Planning Instead of Functional Relief

The audience may like pretty tools, but they buy relief.

**Avoid leading with:** Beautiful pages, cozy desk scenes, perfect routines
**Lead with:** Less overwhelm, easier starting, fewer abandoned planners, low-energy options, resetting after falling behind

### 9.3 Overloading the Pin

ADHD viewers need fast comprehension.

**Avoid:** Tiny text, too many fonts, too many competing elements, overly long headlines, dense paragraphs on the image

### 9.4 Using Shame-Based Productivity Language

**Avoid:** Lazy, excuses, discipline, "no more procrastination forever", fix your ADHD
**Better:** Stuck, overwhelmed, low-energy, reset, start smaller, reduce friction

### 9.5 Making Medical Claims

**Avoid:** "Cures executive dysfunction", "Fixes ADHD", "Guaranteed to stop procrastination"
**Better:** "Designed for ADHD planning struggles", "Helps reduce planning friction", "Supports task initiation"

### 9.6 Hiding the Differentiation

PNP should not look like every printable planner brand. Always consider whether to include: browser-based, reset tool, not another printable, built for planner abandonment, low-energy mode.

### 9.7 Using AI-Generated Images

**Pinterest's algorithm is actively cracking down on AI-generated images.** Use real product screenshots, mockups, and authentic imagery. This is especially important for long-term strategy.

## 12. FINAL RULE FOR SCRIBE

Every PNP pin should answer one of these questions immediately:

- "What do I do when I cannot start?"
- "How do I reset when I already fell behind?"
- "How do I plan without overwhelming myself?"
- "What tool works when planners do not?"
- "How do I reduce friction instead of relying on willpower?"

**If the pin does not answer one of those, it is probably too generic for Paper N Pixels.**
