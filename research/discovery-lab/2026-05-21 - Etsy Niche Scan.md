# Etsy Niche Scan — 2026-05-21

## Why this scan exists

Kyle wants a proven Etsy side-income path that can be substantially operated with Theo/AI/subagents. The brief is to avoid jewellery and anything difficult to replicate. Stress level should not be used as a filter. The focus is: proven demand, profitability signals, ethical replicability, and whether AI can help produce, list, manage, and iterate the shop with Kyle mainly overseeing taste and direction.

## Sources checked

- Marketplace Pulse: Top Etsy sellers, updated 2026-05-21.
- EtsyHunt/EHunt: Top 100 Etsy Shops 2026, updated 2026-05-01.
- EHunt: Best-selling digital downloads on Etsy 2026, updated 2026-05-01.
- Printful: 13 trending Etsy niches to explore in 2026.
- Printify: Top-selling niches on Etsy in 2026.
- Direct Etsy/EHunt shop pages for MyPorchPrints, DigitalCurio, HeatherRobertsArt, SamPooleDesigns, GingerJournals.

## Market-level findings

### 1. Craft Supplies & Tools is the strongest Etsy category by top-seller count

Marketplace Pulse shows Craft Supplies & Tools dominates the top 100 Etsy sellers: 50 of the top 100 shops are in this category. That category includes many physical supplies, but it also contains highly replicable digital products like SVGs, PNGs, clipart, sublimation files, Cricut files, digital craft papers, fonts, and design bundles.

This is the strongest broad category, but we should avoid physical supply shops like beads, charms, stones, and fabric unless there is a digital-only angle.

### 2. Paper & Party Supplies has fewer mega-shops but stronger digital-printable fit

Paper & Party Supplies only accounts for 5 of the top 100 on Marketplace Pulse, but the shops that do well here are much closer to Kyle's likely lane: printable kits, junk journals, invitations, party templates, tags, cards, printable papers, and digital ephemera.

This category feels more replicable than physical craft supplies because the product can be designed once and delivered instantly.

### 3. Digital downloads are the clearest AI-operable path

EHunt's best-selling digital downloads list shows weekly demand around:

- junk journal printables and vintage ephemera
- PNG / sublimation design files
- embroidery files
- SVG / Cricut files
- cross-stitch patterns
- PLR / MRR bundles

The top 10 digital download examples were not huge single-product numbers, but they prove active buyer demand. The big money appears to come from a large catalogue, bundles, repeat launches, and owning a very clear aesthetic lane.

## Niches worth deeper investigation

### A. Junk journal / vintage ephemera printables

Evidence:

- MyPorchPrints: around 1.14M total sales, around 11k 30-day sales, around 1,379 products according to EHunt.
- EHunt's #1 best-selling digital download was a vintage ephemera paper pack from SamPooleDesigns.
- GingerJournals has around 103k total sales, around 1.9k 30-day sales, and a 930-kit whole-shop bundle model.

Why it might work:

- Digital delivery.
- Visual style can be systematised into themed kits.
- Buyers expect bundles, add-ons, and repeat collections.
- Strong overlap with Pinterest and YouTube craft audiences.

Risks:

- Needs taste and visual cohesion.
- Risk of generic AI slop if not curated well.
- Vintage/public-domain assets need clean rights handling.

Initial verdict: strongest candidate for a calm, replicable Etsy experiment.

### B. SVG / PNG / sublimation craft files

Evidence:

- HeatherRobertsArt: around 1.09M total sales, 3.5k+ Etsy-listed items, 4.9 average review, digital downloads focused on SVG, PNG, sublimation, fonts, tumbler wraps and bundles.
- DigitalCurio: around 925k total sales, around 6.4k products, commercial-use graphics and clipart.
- EHunt digital download list includes PNG, sublimation, SVG and Cricut files.

Why it might work:

- Extremely scalable.
- Low fulfilment burden.
- Can test fast around seasons and micro-trends.
- Buyers already search by use-case: Cricut, t-shirt, tumbler, teacher, Easter, Christmas, coquette, western, Christian, etc.

Risks:

- Very competitive.
- Requires either high catalogue volume or a sharp niche angle.
- Trend-chasing can become operationally noisy unless subagents handle monitoring and Kyle only approves direction.
- IP/copyright traps are everywhere.

Initial verdict: viable, especially if narrowed into one AI-operable lane with strict IP rules rather than broad random trend-chasing.

### C. Planner stickers / printable organisation kits

Evidence:

- PlannerKate1 is top-tier by lifetime Etsy sales: around 2.07M sales on Marketplace Pulse/EHunt.
- Printful flags personalised notebooks, journals, planners and printable planner kits as a trending niche.

Why it might work:

- Good overlap with Kyle's existing ADHD/productivity thinking.
- Digital planner inserts, printable trackers, routine cards, and sticker sheets are easy to make.
- Could reuse some PNP learning without carrying the whole emotional weight of the PNP project.

Risks:

- PlannerKate1 appears to be a major established brand with many physical planner stickers and operational complexity.
- Competing head-on would be a bad idea.

Initial verdict: do not replicate PlannerKate directly. Consider a small digital-only sub-niche if we keep it light.

## Niches to avoid for this brief

- Jewellery: top-performing but explicitly out of scope and not easy to replicate.
- Beads/physical craft supplies: proven but inventory-heavy.
- Wedding/bridesmaid gifts: proven but fulfilment, personalisation, and customer-service heavy.
- Seeds/plants/incense/home goods: proven but logistics-heavy.
- Trademark-heavy pop culture files: demand exists but legal risk is not worth it.

## Revised opportunity filter

Do not rank opportunities by Kyle's personal stress level. Rank by:

- sales/profit signal
- AI-operability: can Theo/AI help create the product, mockups, listing copy, SEO, variations and updates?
- fulfilment simplicity: digital delivery, POD, or simple supplier workflow
- catalogue repeatability: can one product pattern become 20-100 listings?
- differentiation potential: can we create our own version without copying designs?
- operations fit: can subagents monitor trends, generate drafts, prepare listings, and flag decisions for Kyle?

## First-pass opportunity ranking

1. SVG/PNG/sublimation and design-file shops — strongest AI-operability and scalable catalogue model, but needs tight IP rules and a specific lane.
2. Junk journal / vintage ephemera printables — proven demand, digital delivery, repeatable kit architecture, strong AI-assisted production potential if rights are handled properly.
3. Print-on-demand niche shops via Printify — worth deeper research because AI can assist product concepts, artwork variants, mockups, listing copy and SEO, while fulfilment is outsourced.
4. Digital-only planner / organisation printables — familiar territory, but should only continue if the niche shows profit signals beyond PNP assumptions.

## Research gaps before building anything

- Check current Etsy search result saturation for the top 3 micro-niches.
- Pull 20 best-selling listings in each lane and classify title structure, image style, price, bundle size, and review complaints.
- Check Pinterest demand for visual niches, especially junk journal printables.
- Decide whether we want a faceless brand, a cosy craft persona, or a practical utility brand.
