# 2026-05-22 - Boring Utility and Checker Website Scan

**Last updated:** 2026-05-22

## Brief

Kyle wants a less glamorous, more realistic side-income direction than stressful shop/product work. The working model is to reverse-engineer proven small web businesses and build simpler, narrower versions using his unused Hostinger site capacity.

Two tracks:

- **Track A — Structured checkers:** guided checklist/report tools where the value is knowing the right questions to ask.
- **Track B — Boring utility / lead-gen sites:** calculators, estimators, local quote tools, and narrow SEO pages that capture commercial intent.

Important constraint: avoid legal/tax/GDPR overclaiming. These should be framed as readiness tools, admin checklists, estimates, or preparation aids, not professional advice or compliance certification.

## Hostinger implication

Kyle has around 50 unused Hostinger websites. That materially changes the opportunity. The sensible play is not one big SaaS. It is a portfolio of small, narrow tests:

1. pick a specific pain/search intent;
2. build one simple checker/calculator;
3. add a useful guide below it for SEO;
4. add privacy/disclaimer pages;
5. capture email/lead intent;
6. validate with search console, outreach, and basic competitor checks;
7. only then deepen the site or add local pages.

The realistic target is first **£50-£150/month per winning micro-site**, not £10k/month from a guru-style lead-gen funnel.

## Track A — Structured checker opportunities

### 1. Making Tax Digital readiness checker

**Concept:** A UK sole trader/landlord checker that asks income type, approximate qualifying income, Self Assessment status, current recordkeeping method, and software readiness. Output: whether MTD may apply soon, what to check on GOV.UK, and a preparation checklist.

**Why it beats generic ChatGPT:** the user does not know the sequence of questions. The tool can force the right flow and produce an action list.

**Monetisation:** accounting software affiliate, accountant/bookkeeper lead-gen, paid MTD prep checklist.

**Sources/examples:**

- GOV.UK MTD guidance/checker: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/find-out-if-and-when-you-need-to-use-making-tax-digital-for-income-tax

**Verdict:** High fit. Timely, UK-specific, simple logic, clear affiliate path. Needs ongoing threshold/date updates.

### 2. UK website legal/GDPR checker for tiny businesses

**Concept:** Tool asks about contact forms, analytics, cookies, email signup, payments, embedded videos, client data, and marketing pixels. Output: likely missing website pages/notices/actions.

**Why it beats generic ChatGPT:** micro-business owners need a structured triage, not a broad GDPR lecture.

**Monetisation:** affiliate to cookie/privacy tools, paid legal page starter pack, lead-gen to GDPR consultants.

**Sources/examples:**

- ICO small business assessment: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/advice-for-small-organisations/getting-started-with-gdpr/assessment-for-small-business-owners-and-sole-traders/
- ICO cookies/privacy guidance: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/advice-for-small-organisations/privacy-notices-and-cookies/cookies-and-privacy-notices-in-detail/
- Termly privacy policy generator: https://termly.io/products/privacy-policy-generator/
- TermsFeed generator: https://www.termsfeed.com/privacy-policy-generator/
- CookieYes generator: https://www.cookieyes.com/privacy-policy-generator/

**Verdict:** High fit. Good Hostinger portfolio candidate because it can spawn sector pages: photographer, coach, therapist, Etsy seller, local trades, consultant.

### 3. Self-employed records / expenses prep checker

**Concept:** Tool asks about invoices, receipts, mileage, home-working, accounting method, subscriptions, phone/internet, business/personal separation, and outputs missing records before Self Assessment.

**Monetisation:** accounting software affiliate, paid tax-year checklist, accountant/bookkeeper referral.

**Sources/examples:**

- GOV.UK self-employed records: https://www.gov.uk/self-employed-records
- GOV.UK simplified expenses: https://www.gov.uk/simpler-income-tax-simplified-expenses
- GOV.UK simplified expenses checker: https://www.gov.uk/simplified-expenses-checker
- FreeAgent Self Assessment guide: https://www.freeagent.com/guides/self-assessment/list-of-things-you-need-to-complete-self-assessment/

**Verdict:** High fit. Evergreen and low build complexity. Must avoid personalised tax advice.

### 4. First employee / new employer setup checker

**Concept:** “I’m hiring my first employee. What admin do I need?” Covers PAYE, payroll, right-to-work, employment contract, employer’s liability insurance, pension auto-enrolment, communications, and deadlines.

**Monetisation:** payroll/HR software affiliate, accountant/payroll bureau lead-gen, paid first-hire admin pack.

**Sources/examples:**

- Pensions Regulator new employer duties: https://www.thepensionsregulator.gov.uk/en/business-advisers/automatic-enrolment-guide-for-business-advisers/duties-for-new-employers
- GOV.UK workplace pensions: https://www.gov.uk/workplace-pensions/joining-a-workplace-pension

**Verdict:** High fit but slightly more liability-sensitive. Strong pain point because first-time employers are anxious and deadline-driven.

### 5. Late invoice payment calculator + chaser generator

**Concept:** B2B overdue invoice calculator using UK statutory interest rules, plus generated polite/firm chasing emails.

**Monetisation:** paid email/letter pack, invoice software affiliate, debt collection/referral.

**Sources/examples:**

- GOV.UK late commercial payments: https://www.gov.uk/late-commercial-payments-interest-debt-recovery/charging-interest-commercial-debt
- ContractorCalculator late payment calculator: https://www.contractorcalculator.co.uk/late_payment_calculator.aspx
- DontPayLate: https://dontpaylate.uk/
- Landolio late payment calculator: https://landolio.com/tools/late-payment-interest-calculator.html

**Verdict:** Medium-high fit. Easiest MVP. Existing calculators exist, but most do not package the next-step scripts well.

### 6. Freelancer contract red-flag checker

**Concept:** Upload/paste contract text, then run a structured checklist across payment terms, IP ownership, liability, indemnity, termination, cancellation, scope creep, revisions, portfolio rights, non-compete, governing law, GDPR/data handling, and dispute process.

**Positioning:** not “AI legal review”. Better: “plain-English contract risk spotting and negotiation prep for freelancers”.

**Monetisation:** paid report, negotiation script pack, premium checklist templates, solicitor referral only where appropriate.

**Verdict:** Interesting but higher risk than calculators. Best as a later Track A product after proving the simpler checker pattern.

## Track B — Boring utility / lead-gen opportunities

### 1. Fencing cost calculator

**Concept:** Estimate fence/gate costs based on panels, height, material, removal, access, gates, and postcode/region. CTA: get local fencing quotes.

**Existing proof/examples:**

- DIY My Home fencing cost calculator: https://diy-my-home.co.uk/fencing-cost-calculator

**Traffic intent:** “fencing cost calculator”, “garden fence cost per panel”, “new fence cost UK”, “fencing quotes near me”.

**Monetisation:** per-lead fee, monthly local featured installer, local directory sponsorship.

**Verdict:** Best beginner Track B candidate. Low compliance risk, easy calculator, many small contractors, boring enough to be overlooked.

### 2. Driveway / patio cost calculator

**Concept:** Estimate tarmac/resin/gravel/block paving/concrete driveway or patio cost from m², surface, existing base, drainage/access, and region. CTA: compare local installers.

**Existing proof/examples:**

- MyJobQuote driveway cost guide: https://www.myjobquote.co.uk/costs/new-driveway
- MyHouseProject driveway calculator: https://myhouseproject.co.uk/driveway-cost-calculator
- Paving Quoter: https://pavingquoter.co.uk
- Checkatrade tarmac driveway calculator: https://www.checkatrade.com/blog/cost-guides/tarmac-driveway-cost-calculator

**Traffic intent:** “driveway cost calculator”, “resin driveway cost”, “tarmac driveway cost UK”, “block paving prices”.

**Monetisation:** higher-value local leads, paid installer placements, sponsorship.

**Verdict:** Strong but more competitive. Good if localised by town/county.

### 3. Skip size / skip permit calculator

**Concept:** “What size skip do I need?” plus “Do I need a permit in my council area?” Capture postcode, waste type, size, and urgency.

**Existing proof/examples:**

- MyJobQuote skip hire guide: https://www.myjobquote.co.uk/costs/skip-hire

**Traffic intent:** “skip hire prices”, “what size skip do I need”, “mini skip cost”, “skip permit cost [council]”.

**Monetisation:** local skip hire referrals, call tracking, lead-gen, possibly ads later.

**Verdict:** Very boring, useful, and scalable locally. Lead value lower than driveways but traffic intent is clear.

### 4. EICR certificate cost calculator

**Concept:** Landlord/homeowner tool estimating EICR cost by property type, bedrooms, circuits if known, urgency, and location. CTA: local NICEIC/NAPIT electricians.

**Traffic intent:** “EICR cost calculator”, “EICR certificate cost”, “landlord electrical certificate cost”.

**Monetisation:** electrician leads, landlord email list, local directory/sponsorship.

**Verdict:** Strong commercial intent and recurring compliance. Must avoid technical safety advice and always route to qualified electricians.

### 5. Worktop replacement calculator

**Concept:** Estimate kitchen worktop replacement cost by material, run length, cut-outs, backsplash/upstand, removal, and region.

**Existing proof/examples:**

- BestBuilders worktop cost calculator: https://bestbuilders.co.uk/costs/worktop-cost-calculator

**Traffic intent:** “worktop cost calculator UK”, “quartz worktop cost”, “granite worktop price”, “kitchen worktop replacement cost”.

**Monetisation:** worktop supplier/fitter leads, kitchen installer referrals, paid local listings.

**Verdict:** Good because it is high-ticket but less regulated than boilers/electrics/legal.

### 6. Spray foam removal calculator

**Concept:** Estimate spray foam removal cost by property/loft size, foam type, access, and urgency. CTA: vetted removal survey.

**Existing proof/examples:**

- Spray Foam Removal Advice calculator: https://sprayfoamremovaladvice.co.uk/calculator

**Traffic intent:** “spray foam removal cost UK”, “spray foam mortgage problem”, “spray foam loft insulation removal”.

**Monetisation:** specialist removal leads.

**Verdict:** Interesting but ethically sensitive because the niche is fear-led. Needs careful copy and source-backed claims.

### 7. Garden clearance / hedge trimming / lawn mowing calculator

**Concept:** Cluster of low-risk gardening calculators: garden clearance, hedge trimming, lawn mowing, green waste removal.

**Traffic intent:** “garden clearance cost”, “hedge trimming cost”, “lawn mowing prices UK”.

**Monetisation:** gardener leads, local sponsored profile, directory listings.

**Verdict:** Low risk and easy, but lower lead value. Good as a calm test site.

## Initial ranking for Kyle

### Best first tests

1. **Fencing cost calculator** — best mix of boring, low risk, local lead potential, and easy build.
2. **MTD readiness checker** — best UK-specific Track A opportunity with clear timing and software affiliate path.
3. **Website legal/GDPR checker** — strong fit for micro-businesses and sector landing pages.
4. **Driveway/patio cost calculator** — more money per lead, but more competition.
5. **Late invoice calculator + chaser generator** — easiest MVP and strongly freelancer-aligned.

### Good later candidates

- EICR cost calculator
- Worktop replacement calculator
- First employee checker
- Self-employed records checker
- Freelancer contract red-flag checker
- Skip permit/size calculator

### Avoid first

- National boiler/solar marketplaces: high value, but competitive and trust-heavy.
- Conveyancing comparison: attractive but higher legal/compliance trust burden.
- Mortgage/stamp duty calculators: giant/authority dominated and YMYL-heavy.
- Anything that requires pretending to have a national contractor network.

## Reverse-engineering workflow for future scans

Use this 20-minute validation per competitor/domain:

1. **Site snapshot:** What tool is it? What CTA? What monetisation is visible?
2. **Traffic estimate:** Similarweb, Ahrefs traffic checker, SEMrush free domain overview.
3. **SEO check:** Google main keyword, long-tail variations, autocomplete, People Also Ask, Keyword Surfer/Ubersuggest/Keywords Everywhere.
4. **Ads check:** Google Ads Transparency Center, Meta Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center, LinkedIn Ad Library for B2B.
5. **Backlinks:** Ahrefs free backlink checker, Moz/SEMrush limited, Google `"domain.com" -site:domain.com` searches.
6. **Monetisation inspection:** visible ads, forms, affiliate links, `/ads.txt`, Wappalyzer/BuiltWith/WhatRuns, source search for `adsbygoogle`, `googlesyndication`, `awin`, `impact`, `shareasale`, `partnerize`, `fbq`, `gtag`.
7. **Replicability score:** traffic proof, SEO difficulty, build difficulty, data/API dependency, monetisation clarity, differentiation opportunity, legal/compliance risk.

Classify each as:

- **Clone/improve now** — visible demand, weak enough competition, simple build, clear money path.
- **Niche down** — broad keyword competitive but local/sector long-tails possible.
- **Inspiration only** — good model but too hard to rank or monetise directly.
- **Skip** — no traffic proof, unclear monetisation, high liability, or giant-dominated SERP.

## Practical MVP structure for a Hostinger site

Each test site should have:

- calculator/checker landing page;
- result screen with low/high estimate or checklist score;
- “what affects the result” guide under the tool;
- FAQ section targeting People Also Ask queries;
- lead capture form or email capture;
- privacy policy, terms, disclaimer;
- 5-10 supporting articles;
- 5-20 local/sector pages only where genuinely useful;
- Google Search Console and analytics;
- basic outreach list of potential partners before spending too much time building.

## Key strategic point

The video’s broad “AI lead-gen website” pitch is too optimistic. But the underlying pattern is useful if narrowed:

> Build tiny, boring, useful tools that sit in front of commercial intent.

Kyle’s Hostinger capacity makes a portfolio test realistic. The danger is building 50 thin sites. The better move is to build **one polished micro-site first**, prove search/lead/outreach mechanics, then templatise the build pattern.
