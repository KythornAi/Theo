# Three Model Etsy Comparison — 2026-05-21

## Brief

Compare three Etsy shop models for AI-operable replication:

1. SVG / PNG / sublimation / design-file shops
2. Printify / print-on-demand niche shops
3. Printable / junk journal / ephemera shops

Kyle's stress level is not a ranking factor. The key question is whether Theo/AI/subagents can help run the model and whether the model has profit potential.

## Profitability data caveat

Public data is directional, not exact. EHunt/EtsyHunt, Alura, EverBee, eRank and similar tools estimate sales/revenue differently. Etsy does not expose true profit. For now I captured:

- shop names
- public sales signals
- 30-day sales where EHunt exposed it
- listing revenue where Alura/EHunt exposed it
- rough revenue/profit proxies for comparison

For proper validation later, check these shops/listings in EverBee, ProfitTree, Alura or eRank.

## Rough profit model used here

These are not exact profits. They are scenario estimates only.

Digital/download shops:

- Assumed gross margin proxy: 85% after Etsy/payment fees and light tool costs, before ads/tax.
- AOV scenarios vary because prices range from cheap single files to bundles.

Print-on-demand shops:

- Printify recommends a 40-50% margin, but real Etsy/POD margins after production, shipping assumptions, Etsy fees, discounts, ads and refunds can be lower.
- I used 35% gross profit proxy for conservative comparison.

### AI-operability

Very high.

Theo/AI/subagents can help with:

- niche and keyword monitoring
- design concept generation
- style-board creation
- prompt generation for original artwork
- mockup planning
- Etsy titles, tags, descriptions, FAQs
- bundle architecture
- seasonal production calendars
- listing upload checklists

### Profitability potential

High, but depends on catalogue scale and niche selection. The best-looking part of this model is the high digital margin and repeatable listing structure.

### Main risks

- Copyright/trademark risk, especially pop culture, characters, bands, quotes and fandom content.

- Large catalogue expectation.

- commercial-use journal graphics for paper crafters
- teacher/classroom SVG bundles with original sayings
- faith-neutral affirmation graphics
- botanical/cottagecore clipart packs
- pet-owner quote PNGs with original artwork
- seasonal sublimation packs with strict IP checks

## Side-by-side verdict

### 1st place for profit signal: Printable / junk journal / ephemera

Reason: strongest 30-day sales signal from MyPorchPrints and still strong smaller-shop signals from GingerJournals and SamPooleDesigns. Digital margins are attractive. AI can help, but taste/rights need managing.

### 1st place for AI-operability: SVG / PNG / sublimation / design files

Reason: highly repeatable, digital delivery, strong shop examples, clear listing mechanics. It may be the best fit for Theo/subagent operations if we choose a safe micro-lane.

### 1st place for fulfilment outsourcing: Printify/POD

Reason: Printify can handle production and shipping, and AI can help with concepts/listings/mockups. But margins are thinner and customer service is heavier. Best if we find a strong niche, not generic shirts.

## Additional factor from Kyle — delivery risk

Kyle flagged concern about dropshipping/POD/delivery-dependent models because shipping, fuel prices, global disruption, delays and fulfilment issues could make those models harder to manage. This does not automatically eliminate Printify/POD, but it should be scored as a real operational risk.

Digital models reduce those worries because there is no shipping, no supplier delay, no postal dispute, no lost parcel, and fewer customer-service variables. For now, digital products should probably get a fulfilment-risk advantage over POD unless the POD niche has much stronger profit proof.
