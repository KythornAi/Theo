# Replicable Shop Shortlist — 2026-05-21

## Replication filter

Score shops by whether their model can be copied ethically as a pattern, not copied as designs. Do not use Kyle's stress level as a deciding factor. The priority is whether Theo/AI/subagents can substantially operate the model and whether the shop type looks profit-capable.

Good signs:

- digital products
- print-on-demand compatibility
- AI can help create product concepts, artwork variants, mockups, listing copy, tags, SEO and operations checklists
- subagents can monitor niches, prepare listings, and flag decisions for Kyle
- repeatable collection format
- clear visual/audience niche
- bundles and add-ons
- no physical fulfilment
- no jewellery/manufacturing dependency

Bad signs:

- inventory
- personalised fulfilment at scale
- massive catalogue needed before any sales
- legal/IP risk
- product requires skill or tooling AI cannot realistically cover
- product requires Kyle to personally do most of the ongoing operation

## Shortlist

### 1. MyPorchPrints — strongest model to study

Data points:

- Category: Paper & Party Supplies.
- EHunt: around 1,146,456 total sales.
- EHunt: around 11,042 30-day sales.
- EHunt: around 1,379 products.
- Etsy search snippet: digital printables for junk journals, junk journal kits, printable papers, tags, cards, vintage ephemera. They do not print or ship items.

What they seem to sell:

- junk journal kits
- printable paper packs
- tags and cards
- vintage ephemera
- themed digital craft kits

Why this is replicable:

- Digital-only.
- Product architecture is repeatable: theme → paper pack → tags → pockets/cards → bundle → mega-bundle.
- Does not require jewellery, stock, shipping, or custom production.
- Aesthetic curation matters more than technical complexity.

What not to copy:

- Specific art, layouts, brand look, listing images, names, or bundles.

Possible ethical replication angle:

- Build a new shop around one under-served emotional/aesthetic lane, e.g. "quiet cottage archive", "cosy ADHD memory keeping", "Victorian garden letters", "rainy-day journaling kits", or "printable comfort craft kits".

AI-operability note: high. Theo/AI can generate theme concepts, kit structures, listing drafts, tags, SEO variants, mockup plans, bundle strategy and production checklists. Kyle's role would be taste approval and direction, not hand-making every asset.

Verdict: strong candidate, but should compete against SVG/PNG and POD options using the revised scoring filter.

### 2. GingerJournals — bundle/catalogue model

Data points:

- Category: Paper & Party Supplies.
- EHunt: around 103,769 total sales.
- EHunt: around 1,901 30-day sales.
- EHunt: around 930 products.
- Etsy listing/search data shows a whole-shop bundle of junk journal printables, journal pages, kits, digital downloads, pages and ephemera.

What they seem to sell:

- printable journal pages
- ephemera
- digital journal kits
- whole-shop bundle model

Why this is replicable:

- Shows a smaller but still serious shop can get traction in this niche.
- The catalogue/bundle model is clear and repeatable.
- Whole-shop bundles create high perceived value once there is enough inventory.

Risks:

- Needs a lot of products before the bundle model becomes convincing.
- If created too generically, it becomes low-quality AI-looking printables.

Possible replication angle:

- Start with 10 coherent kits, not hundreds. Build toward bundles once the design language is proven.

AI-operability note: high for bundle/listing operations, medium for design quality. Theo/AI can build bundle maps, listing templates and production schedules, but the visual standard must be actively curated.

Verdict: good model, especially if we validate a smaller lane before scaling.

### 3. SamPooleDesigns — high-signal micro shop in digital ephemera

Data points:

- Category: Paper & Party Supplies.
- EHunt: around 31,242 total sales.
- EHunt: around 1,029 30-day sales.
- EHunt: around 128 products.
- EHunt's #1 weekly digital download was a SamPooleDesigns vintage ephemera A4 paper pack with 63 weekly sales, 216 total sales, and $1,166 listed revenue.

What they seem to sell:

- vintage-style digital ephemera
- paper packs
- journal pages / mixed media assets

Why this is especially useful:

- Lower product count than the million-sale giants, but still active 30-day sales.
- This is closer to an attainable model than trying to replicate a 1,000+ product catalogue immediately.

Risks:

- Vintage/mixed-media buyers have taste. Low-effort designs will likely fail.

Possible replication angle:

- Curated themed paper packs with a distinctive emotional story, not random vintage collage.

AI-operability note: high. This is useful because it has active demand with a much smaller catalogue. Theo/AI can help generate pack concepts, paper variants, listing drafts and image/mockup plans, while Kyle curates the final look.

Verdict: realistic shop pattern to model for a first test, but not automatically better than SVG/PNG or POD.

### 4. HeatherRobertsArt — scalable SVG/PNG model

Data points:

- Category: Craft Supplies & Tools.
- Etsy: around 1,092,554 sales, 4.9 average rating, 81k+ reviews.
- Etsy: around 3,516 listed items in visible shop data.
- EHunt: around 4,375 products and 1,260 30-day sales.
- Sells SVG, PNG, clipart, sublimation files, tumbler wraps, fonts, bundles and commercial licences.

What they seem to sell:

- Cricut/Silhouette SVG files
- sublimation PNGs
- tumbler wraps
- design bundles
- seasonal and themed craft graphics

Why this is replicable:

- Digital-only.
- Clear use cases and search keywords.
- Repeatable seasonal production calendar.

Operational risks:

- The catalogue is huge.
- Trend chasing could turn into a treadmill.
- This niche has heavy competition and copyright traps.

Possible replication angle:

- Do not make a general SVG shop at first. Pick one AI-operable sub-lane with strong demand, e.g. printable craft files for journalers, tumbler wraps for a specific audience, teacher SVG bundles, or seasonal sublimation packs with strict IP checks.

AI-operability note: very high. Theo/AI can generate trend maps, concept lists, artwork variants, mockups, listing titles, tags, descriptions and production calendars. This may be one of the strongest models if we control IP risk and avoid copying.

Verdict: should be studied seriously and may rank above printables after a listing-level teardown.

### 5. DigitalCurio — commercial-use clipart/catalogue model

Data points:

- Category: Craft Supplies & Tools.
- EHunt: around 925,276 total sales.
- EHunt: around 2,009 30-day sales.
- EHunt: around 6,394 products.
- Search snippet: commercial-use clipart and graphics, POD-friendly.

What they seem to sell:

- commercial-use clipart
- graphics packs
- vintage/celestial/floral designs
- POD-friendly assets

Why this is replicable:

- Digital-only and scalable.
- Commercial-use licence gives buyers a business reason to purchase.
- Strong overlap with creators, crafters, and other Etsy sellers.

Risks:

- Very large catalogue.
- Needs consistent art style and licensing clarity.
- Harder to differentiate if AI-generated clipart floods the category.

Possible replication angle:

- Create a smaller, tightly themed asset library for one customer: journal makers, homeschool mums, cottagecore sellers, ADHD planner creators, etc.

AI-operability note: high. Theo/AI can help create asset themes, product naming, previews, licence wording, listing SEO, and bundle architecture. The hard part is quality control and rights clarity, not day-to-day listing operations.

Verdict: useful long-term model and worth comparing directly with SVG/PNG and POD.

## Revised first experiment logic

The best first experiment should be chosen by AI-operability and profit signal, not by whether it feels low-stress.

Potential experiment types:

1. AI-assisted SVG/PNG/sublimation micro-shop.
2. AI-assisted printable journal/ephemera shop.
3. Printify/POD micro-brand where Theo handles concepts, mockups, listing drafts and SEO, while Kyle approves taste.

## Previous first experiment idea

The earlier recommendation favoured printable-journal products partly because they looked low-stress. Keep the data, but do not treat low-stress as the reason to choose it. The product should win only if it scores well on profit signal, AI-operability, fulfilment simplicity and differentiation.

Recommended MVP:

- 1 shop concept.
- 10 listings.
- 3 visual themes.
- Each theme has: paper pack, tags/cards, journal pages, pockets/envelopes, mini bundle.
- Price: low-ticket digital downloads and one bundle.
- Goal: prove that strangers click/favourite/buy before scaling.

## Candidate shop concepts

### Concept A: The Cosy Archive

Printable vintage-inspired journaling kits for people who want comforting, rainy-day craft sessions.

Pros:

- Fits junk journal market.
- Calm brand feeling.
- No ADHD baggage unless we choose to add it later.

Cons:

- Needs strong visual taste.

### Concept B: Small Calm Papers

Printable paper kits for quiet crafts, memory keeping, and low-pressure journaling.

Pros:

- Aligns with Kyle's AI-overseen direction: Theo can prepare concepts/listings and Kyle can approve taste.
- Could lightly connect to ADHD without being a productivity brand.

Cons:

- Positioning needs care so it does not feel vague.

### Concept C: Printable Nook

Simple themed printables for journaling, scrapbooking, pen pals, and paper crafts.

Pros:

- Broad enough to expand.
- Easy to understand.

Cons:

- Less distinctive.
