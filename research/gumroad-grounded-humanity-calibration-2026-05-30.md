## Practical patterns from the market

### 1. Price ranges and bundle signals

- **Standalone printable affirmation cards:** roughly **$3 / £1.78–£3** at the low end, often driven by quantity and printable convenience rather than brand voice.
- **Small guided workbooks/journals:** often **£5 / $11.99–$15** when they include 27–44 pages, prompts, trackers, and printable/digital PDF use.
- **Larger activity/workbook/card bundles:** **$15–$19** for themed bundles, **$59** for all-access mega-bundles.
- **Premium narrative PDF product:** the strongest example was **Slow Mornings at $40**, but it relies on distinctive writing, page count, strong aesthetic, clear daily structure, and an emotionally precise audience fit.

**Implication for Kyle:** do not price against cheap card decks alone. The offer is an ebook + ADHD card deck, so £9 launch / £12 standard sits between cheap printable cards and larger $15–$19 bundles. It avoids looking like generic PLR while staying low-friction for a first Gumroad product.

### 2. Title/subtitle styles

Common title patterns:
- Quantity + format + benefit: “40 Printable Mindfulness Worksheets & Scripts For Adults.”
- Emotional outcome + format: “Anxiety & Stress Relief Journal – Guided Self-Care & Mindfulness Planner.”
- Brand/narrative title + concrete promise: “Slow Mornings — a quieter way to wake.”
- SEO-heavy marketplace titles: long strings with “printable,” “PDF,” “mindfulness,” “self-care,” “therapy office decor,” etc.

**Implication for Kyle:** lead with the distinctive book title, then add practical search terms. Avoid keyword-stuffed Etsy-style titles on Gumroad. Gumroad can support a cleaner title plus a descriptive subtitle.

### 3. Screenshots/previews shown

Strong examples show:
- Cover/mockup first.
- 3–5 interior screenshots or page previews.
- A visual of the card deck laid out or cut/printed.
- A contents/inclusions graphic.
- Sometimes a sample/excerpt page.
- File/delivery details near the bottom.

**Implication for Kyle:** the Gumroad page needs visual proof more than more features. The existing Canva cards and Reedsy-formatted ebook should be used as previews.

### 4. Buyer promises

Most competitors promise some mix of:
- Calm, focus, self-awareness, inner peace.
- Stress relief, anxiety support, emotional balance.
- Habit-building, daily reminders, reflection prompts.
- Easy printing, instant download, use-at-your-own-pace.
- Morning/evening routines and reset rituals.

Some claims are too strong for Kyle’s positioning, especially “manage anxiety,” “reduce anxiety,” “healing,” and “therapy office” style framing.

**Safer promise shape for Kyle:**
- “A grounded way to try mindfulness in ordinary life.”
- “Gentle prompts for reflection and reset moments.”
- “Cards you can keep visible when your brain needs a kinder cue.”
- “Self-guided support for overwhelm and daily steadiness.”

### 5. Common inclusions

Frequent inclusions:
- Printable PDF.
- A4 and US Letter versions when available.
- Card dimensions, e.g. 2.5 x 3.5 inches and/or 5 x 7 inches.
- Print-at-home or professional print-shop instructions.
- GoodNotes/Notability/tablet compatibility for workbooks.
- Bonus tracker, challenge, card deck, or sample pack.
- Personal-use terms, digital-product/no-shipping note, no-refund note.

**Implication for Kyle:** include at least the ebook PDF and printable card PDF. If feasible before launch, add simple A4 and US Letter versions for cards. Do not delay launch for GoodNotes/fillable versions unless they already exist.

### 6. Disclaimers and claim-safe wording

Competitor listings often include usage terms and no-refund wording, but not always mental-health disclaimers. Kyle should be more careful because this is wellbeing-adjacent and ADHD-positioned.

Recommended wording:

> This is a self-guided reflection resource for personal use. It is not medical advice, therapy, diagnosis, or treatment. If you are struggling with your mental health or feel unsafe, please seek support from a qualified professional or local crisis service.

Also include:
- Digital product; no physical item shipped.
- For personal use only; do not resell, redistribute, or claim as your own.
- Colours may vary by screen/printer if cards are printable.
- Due to the digital nature of the product, refunds are limited/not normally available, subject to Gumroad policy and customer support discretion.

## 5 concrete page improvements before upload

1. **Add a sharp no-woo above-the-fold promise.** Make the first screen immediately say this is practical mindfulness for real life, not spiritual performance or toxic positivity.
2. **Show the bundle visually.** The listing should make the ebook + 60 cards feel like one coherent mini-bundle, not two random files. Use a combined cover/card mockup as image one.
3. **Add exact file specs.** Gumroad buyers expect clarity: PDF, page count if known, number of cards, print size, A4/US Letter if included, instant digital download, no physical item shipped.
4. **Include a short “how to use it” section.** Give 3 simple use paths: read a chapter, pull one card, use a reset prompt; desk/mirror/bedside use; weekly reflection. This increases perceived utility without adding product scope.
5. **Add claim-safe disclaimer and personal-use terms.** This protects the wellbeing positioning and helps the listing look trustworthy rather than generic PLR.
