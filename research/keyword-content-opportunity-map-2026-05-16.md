## 2. REDDIT-PAIN-POINT KEYWORDS (For Content Hooks)

Language that real ADHDers use — pulled from Reddit threads. Use these verbatim in pin titles, blog posts, and ad copy:

- "I know exactly what to do but I physically cannot start"
- "My brain feels like 47 browser tabs and I don't know which one's playing music"
- "I've tried every planner and I abandon them all after two weeks"
- "I stay up until 2am because it's the only time that feels like mine"
- "Doomscrolling is the only thing that feels accessible when I'm struggling"
- "I don't need more techniques — I need lower friction"
- "Everyone says just break it into smaller steps but that's not the problem"
- "I give up on the whole system because I missed one day"
- "I need something that meets me where I am, not where I should be"
- "I want one place for 'what am I doing?' without the overhead"

## 6. COMPETITOR KEYWORD GAPS

Keywords competitors are NOT targeting (from Etsy + app store analysis):

| Gap | Why It Matters |
|-----|---------------|
| `ADHD reset` vs `ADHD planner` | Everyone optimises for "planner." "Reset" is the emerging category PNP can own. |
| `No setup ADHD planner` | No competitor leads with zero-friction onboarding. |
| `ADHD tool` vs `ADHD product` | "Tool" implies utility, not another thing to manage. |
| `ADHD permission` | Unique PNP concept. Zero competition. |
| `ADHD for people who hate planners` | Targets the abandoned-planner demographic directly. |
| `One-time purchase ADHD tool` | Anti-subscription positioning, no competitor owns this. |
| `ADHD browser tool` | No one is targeting "browser" as a differentiator. |
