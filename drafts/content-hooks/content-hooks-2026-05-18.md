## Hook 1: Schedule the Crash

- **Hook:** Your 2pm crash is not a failure. It's a predictable ADHD event you can plan around.
- **Angle:** Everyone sells "fix your afternoon slump." Nobody sells "accept it and schedule your day around it." This is the dominant Reddit insight from multiple high-engagement threads this week — users are moving from "how do I fix the crash" to "how do I plan my life around the crash." PNP can be the first ADHD brand that validates this instead of selling false promises about beating the 2pm wall.
- **Platform:** Pinterest (carousel pin) / Blog (long-form)
- **CTA:** Save this pin for your afternoon reset routine. Try the Daily Reset Board to build your own crash-proof day.
- **Draft copy:** The 2pm wall is not a productivity failure — it's a predictable ADHD event. Your planning tool should help you schedule around it, not pretend you can push through. Morning for deep work, midday for transition, afternoon for low-demand tasks and recovery. Build your day around how your brain actually works, and stop fighting a crash that was never going to listen to a to-do list.
- **Product opportunity:** Core Daily Reset Board positioning. The afternoon "reset mode" is the key differentiator from every existing ADHD planner.

## Hook 2: The Filler Activity Trap

- **Hook:** Why waking up early makes you later (and what to do instead).
- **Angle:** Counterintuitive ADHD insight from Reddit: getting up early to build buffer time backfires if the filler activity itself triggers hyperfocus. Users described losing 45 minutes to a "quick task" when they only had 10 minutes to kill before an appointment. The market speaks about "time management" and "morning routines" but nobody talks about what ADHDers need *between* the blocks — safe activities that won't swallow attention.
- **Platform:** TikTok (60-second script) / Pinterest (infographic)
- **CTA:** Follow for more counterintuitive ADHD tips. Save this for your "safe list" of filler activities.
- **Draft copy:** You know that feeling when you got ready 20 minutes early, picked up your phone for a "quick scroll," and suddenly you're late to everything? That's the filler activity trap. The ADHD brain needs safe 5-minute, 10-minute, and 15-minute activities that won't trigger hyperfocus. Standing by a window. A single repetitive physical task. A specific podcast that never hooks your attention too deep. Build a list of your own safe fillers, and stop fighting the time-between-time.
- **Product opportunity:** Lead magnet opportunity — a printable "Safe Filler Activities" card deck. Future product could include a "time-between-time" mode in the Daily Reset Board that suggests safe fillers based on how much time you have.

## Hook 3: The Shark Routine — Breaking a Bad Day

- **Hook:** Bad day brain lock? Try the Shark Routine.
- **Angle:** r/adhdwomen's highest-engagement thread this cycle was about recovering from a bad day. The community's most-upvoted tactic: an external signal (a specific hair clip, a new podcast, a physical object) acts as a gear-shift trigger, then you do exactly one task to get a small win. This is not "break it into smaller steps" advice — it's a specific, named, shareable tactic. The ADHD market over-indexes on "how to plan your day" and under-indexes on "what to do when the plan is already broken."
- **Platform:** TikTok (short, punchy) / Pinterest (list-style pin)
- **CTA:** Save this for your next bad day. Check your inbox for a free daily reset guide.
- **Draft copy:** You had a bad day. The spiral is spinning. Before you write the whole day off, try the Shark Routine. Pick one external signal — a specific bracelet you wear, a playlist that only plays on reset days — and let that switch your brain into a new gear. Then pick ONE task, the easiest one you can find, and do it. A small win rewrites the narrative. The rest of the day can wait. You don't need a whole new system. You just need a way to break the freeze.
- **Product opportunity:** Daily Reset Board's "bad day mode" — reduces the board to three bare-minimum items (eat, hydrate, one small win). No tracking, no guilt, just a reset starting point.

## Hook 4: Your App Collection IS the Problem

- **Hook:** Your ADHD productivity app collection is a symptom, not a solution.
- **Angle:** This is the tension from the r/ProductivityApps thread that perfectly captures the market's blind spot. Users are spending more time discovering, evaluating, and switching between productivity tools than actually being productive. One user compiled a list of 60+ app recommendations — the act of compiling itself became the distraction. The blunt framing ("your collection IS the problem") is shareable, honest, and positions PNP as the brand that understands that tools should reduce friction, not add another layer to manage.
- **Platform:** TikTok (provocative opener) / Blog (link in bio)
- **CTA:** Stop researching. Pick one tool that asks nothing from you. Link in bio.
- **Draft copy:** If you have 17 productivity apps on your phone and you still can't start your morning, the pattern is the problem. Every new app promises a fresh start — and every new app gets abandoned by day 14. The ADHD brain turns tool research into its own dopamine loop. What actually works is a single, boring, visible tool that requires zero setup and just sits there waiting for you. Not another app to learn. Not another subscription to manage. Just a place to see what you're doing today and a reason to start.
- **Product opportunity:** Core positioning for PNP's browser-based approach. Single URL, no app store, no GoodNotes integration, no subscription. The anti-collection: one tool, one purpose.

## Hook 5: Lower the Bar, Don't Raise the Discipline

- **Hook:** The ADHD productivity advice nobody is giving you: lower your expectations.
- **Angle:** This is the dominant meta-theme across every source this cycle — Reddit, Pinterest, Etsy reviews. The products that survive with ADHD users are the ones that require nothing except "look at me." The advice that works is "make it easier to start, not harder to quit." Every other ADHD content creator sells "10 habits to transform your life" or "the ultimate daily routine." PNP's counter-position: admit that the bar is already too high. Lower it. That's not giving up — that's strategy.
- **Platform:** Pinterest (text overlay pin) / Blog (foundational brand piece)
- **CTA:** Pin this for the days when your brain says everything is too much. Try the Daily Reset Board — it asks for three things, not twenty.
- **Draft copy:** If your ADHD planner asks you to fill out 15 sections every morning, it's not a planner — it's another job you'll quit by week two. The products that actually work for ADHD brains do one thing: they sit in your eyeline and ask for almost nothing. Three items, not twenty. A visible reminder, not a buried app. A place to reset, not a system to master. Lower the bar. Not because you can't do more, but because starting is the hardest part. Make starting so easy it's harder not to do it.
- **Product opportunity:** Daily Reset Board's core design principle — three tasks max, visible at all times, no setup. Also connects to the "max 3 habits at a time" enforced constraint feature idea from the pain-point harvest.
