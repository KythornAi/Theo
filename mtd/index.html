Common worries

The questions people ask before they start

Is MTD based on profit or turnover?

The threshold is not based on your profit. It is based on gross self-employment and property income before expenses. That is the test HMRC uses. See the full answer →

Do PAYE wages count?

No. Employment income reported through PAYE does not count toward the MTD qualifying-income threshold, though it may still matter for your tax return. See the full answer →

I am both a landlord and a sole trader. Are they combined?

Yes. HMRC adds gross self-employment income and your share of gross property income together to get your qualifying income. See the full answer →

Can I keep using spreadsheets?

You may be able to use spreadsheets with bridging software, but the right setup depends on your records and income sources. See the full answer →

Is a quarterly update the same as a tax return?

No. Quarterly updates are summaries of income and expenses. You still submit one tax return by 31 January following the tax year. See the full answer →

Will you email me or sell my details?

No. The checker gives you an instant result with no email required. Email is optional, only after your result, and only if you tick a box. See the full answer →
