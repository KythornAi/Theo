MTD deadlines: when do you start, and what is due when?

Your MTD for Income Tax start date depends on your qualifying income. Once you are in scope, you send quarterly updates and a final tax return. Here are the key dates.

Start dates

When does MTD for Income Tax start?

HMRC phases the start by qualifying income on a specific tax year's Self Assessment return.

Tax return figuresQualifying incomeMTD start date
2024/25More than £50,0006 April 2026
2025/26More than £30,0006 April 2027
2026/27More than £20,0006 April 2028 (announced, not yet fully legislated)

Source: GOV.UK

If you are in the April 2026 wave, the start date has passed. You should already be keeping digital records. Your first quarterly update is due 7 August 2026. If you have not signed up, read the GOV.UK sign-up guidance now.

Quarterly updates

Quarterly update deadlines (standard periods)

Quarterly updates are summaries of income and expenses, not tax returns. Each update covers from the start of the tax year to the end of the update period. The deadlines below are for standard update periods aligned to the tax year.

Standard update periodUpdate deadline
6 April to 5 July7 August
6 April to 5 October7 November
6 April to 5 January7 February
6 April to 5 April7 May (following the tax year)

Source: GOV.UK: Send quarterly updates

Final declaration

The final tax return deadline stays at 31 January

After your quarterly updates, you still submit one tax return for the tax year. The deadline and payment date remain 31 January following the end of the tax year, as they are now.

Tax yearFinal declaration deadline
2026/2731 January 2028
2027/2831 January 2029
2028/2931 January 2030
Soft landing

Penalty soft landing for 2026/27

GOV.UK says there are no penalty points for late quarterly updates in the 2026/27 tax year. This applies only to the April 2026 cohort. Final declaration and late payment penalties still apply.

From tax years after 2026/27, penalty points will apply for missed quarterly update deadlines. The soft landing is for the first year only. Do not treat it as an ongoing rule.

Source: GOV.UK: Penalties for MTD for Income Tax
