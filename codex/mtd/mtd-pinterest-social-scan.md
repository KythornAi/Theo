# MTD Pinterest and Social Scan

**Date:** 2026-05-25  
**Last updated:** 2026-05-25

## TLDR

- **Verdict: mainly search-led, with selective short-form/social support.** UK MTD for Income Tax is not a naturally inspirational visual topic like planners, interiors or recipes. The strongest demand still points to search utility: “do I need MTD?”, “when do I start?”, “what software do I need?”, “can I still use spreadsheets?”, and landlord/sole-trader threshold confusion. Confidence: **High**.
- **Social discovery is viable as awareness and retargeting, not the MVP acquisition engine.** TikTok, Instagram and YouTube Shorts show search-visible accountants, software providers and banks already using short clips/Reels around MTD, costs, thresholds, landlord/sole-trader changes, “don’t panic” reassurance, and software CTAs. Pinterest has weaker evidence but some pins/boards for landlord/sole-trader tax tips and checklist-style content. Confidence: **Medium**.
- **Best social hooks are practical anxiety reducers:** “Do I need MTD?”, “gross income, not profit”, “sole trader + landlord income is combined”, “quarterly updates are not the same as a tax return”, “can I keep spreadsheets?”, and “no HMRC login needed to check”. Confidence: **High**.
- **Do not prioritise building a big Pinterest/TikTok content machine before the checker and SEO pages exist.** Launch with the search-led checker, then repurpose result branches into pins, Reels, Shorts and Reddit/helpful discussion snippets. Confidence: **High**.

## Evidence by channel

### 1. Pinterest

**Observed evidence**

TinyFish search found a small number of public Pinterest assets for MTD and tax-tip content:

- A UK Pinterest pin titled “Stay Compliant with New UK Digital Tax Rules – Hassle-Free Filing for Sole Traders & Landlords” described UK sole traders or landlords earning over £30,000 needing online submissions through Making Tax Digital.
- A Pinterest board called “UK Sole Trader Tax Tips” included a visible MTD update/tax-tip framing.
- Searches also surfaced pins around “Looking ahead to MTD for landlords” and “Become MTD Ready with Landlord Studio”.

TinyFish fetches were thin. Pinterest pages often returned only titles, short descriptions and generic Pinterest interface text. I could not verify saves, impressions, click-through or meaningful engagement. Treat this as **presence evidence**, not validation of high Pinterest demand.

**Interpretation**

Pinterest can support checklist/search-adjacent assets, but MTD is not naturally a Pinterest-first topic. The viable formats are:

- deadline calendars
- “income before expenses” explainers
- landlord MTD checklist pins
- sole-trader readiness checklist pins
- “what to include/exclude from MTD qualifying income” infographics
- software decision-tree pins

It is unlikely to outperform Google search for bottom-of-funnel checker acquisition at launch.

**Confidence:** Medium for presence; Low for demand strength.

### 2. TikTok

**Observed evidence**

TinyFish search found multiple TikTok pages/videos around MTD:

- `@king_nobez_accountant` with a video titled around hidden MTD costs for sole traders.
- `@gosimpletax` search result: “Making Tax Digital for Income Tax: Essential Guide”, with snippet language aimed at sole traders, landlords and entrepreneurs.
- `@thatahmedkhan`: “Making Tax Digital for Landlords & Sole Traders”, using keywords around MTD Income Tax, Xero, landlords and tax software.
- `@accountant_she`: “Making Tax Digital: What’s Changed & How to Prepare”, with a search snippet showing 1,714 likes and 66 comments. The fetched page also exposed repeated reassurance/ad language: “If you thought Making Tax Digital was going to be pushed back… you’re not alone”, “only just realising it’s here”, “feeling unsure”, “not about doing everything perfectly”, “simple and manageable”, and “Comment MTD and I’ll send you the details.”
- `@rentalbux`: landlord-specific MTD threshold and software videos surfaced in search.
- `@annamoneyuk`: MTD/sole-trader content appeared in search, though the fetched page mismatched to other ANNA Money sole-trader posts, which shows platform fetch instability.

**Interpretation**

TikTok is not giving precise demand metrics through this workflow, but it clearly shows that accountants, apps and property/software brands believe short-form MTD content is worth producing. The strongest pattern is not entertainment. It is creator-led reassurance attached to a specific next step: software trial, comment keyword, membership discount, or explanation.

Useful formats for Kyle:

- “If you are a sole trader or landlord, check these 3 things before April 2026.”
- “MTD is based on gross income, not profit.”
- “Sole trader + landlord? HMRC may combine those income streams.”
- “Quarterly updates are not the same as replacing your tax return.”
- “You do not need your UTR or HMRC login to check your likely start date here.”

**Confidence:** Medium.

### 3. Instagram Reels/posts

**Observed evidence**

Search and fetches found:

- HMRC/GOV.UK Instagram Reel text saying MTD for Income Tax is here, combined turnover from property and self-employment over £50,000 means users need to start keeping digital records, and users should check if MTD applies.
- Starling Instagram Reel: “One tool. Zero drama. Starling’s MTD for Income Tax tool is here”, positioning its tool as free for Starling business customers. A visible comment challenged the “free” wording against a monthly fee, which is useful trust-barrier evidence.
- FreeAgent Instagram post: “Are you a sole trader and a landlord? Feeling confused about some of the logistics of Making Tax Digital for Income Tax? We’ve got you covered.” The visible like count was 18 at fetch time, so this is presence rather than strong engagement proof.
- Other search results surfaced posts aimed at sole traders still using spreadsheets, landlords, and “big changes are coming” framing.

**Interpretation**

Instagram works better as a trust channel than a cold acquisition channel. It can humanise the checker, explain the “not tax advice” boundary, and answer objections. The Starling comment is especially important: users notice and challenge pricing/free claims. Kyle’s landing page should avoid overclaiming free software or hiding affiliate terms.

**Confidence:** Medium.

### 4. YouTube Shorts

**Observed evidence**

Search-visible Shorts included:

- QuickBooks Sole Trader demo for Making Tax Digital.
- Sage Sole Trader App digital record-keeping Short.
- Starling “What is Making Tax Digital?” and “How to use Starling to file MTD returns” Shorts.
- A general “Do I need to worry about MTD?” Short.

TinyFish fetches for Shorts returned only generic YouTube footer text, so I could not read full captions or engagement from the page itself. Perplexity also surfaced a longer YouTube result titled “Making Tax Digital for Income Tax: What Every Sole Trader and Landlord Needs to Do From April 2026”, reinforcing that video demand clusters around practical explanation.

**Interpretation**

Shorts are useful for repurposing exact checker branches into mini explainers. They should send users to the checker rather than trying to teach all MTD rules in-video.

**Confidence:** Medium for presence; Low for performance evidence.

### 5. Reddit and discussion snippets

**Observed evidence**

TinyFish successfully fetched two r/UKPersonalFinance threads:

1. `Making Tax Digital - sole trader AND landlord`  
   The original poster was unclear how MTD works, wanted software, had both self-employed and landlord income, and wondered whether one package could handle both or whether two separate packages were needed. A reply explained combined qualifying income, quarterly updates per income source and one UTR.

2. `Making Tax Digital - software recommendations for Property + Self Employed`  
   The original poster had mixed property and self-employment income, qualified largely because of property income, wanted to try software in readiness, found landlord-only products potentially too much, and wanted a single simple solution rather than over-complication. Comments mentioned people in the same position, spreadsheets and RentalBux trials.

Search snippets also surfaced Reddit posts titled around “Do I need to worry about the Making Tax Digital changes?”, “Making Tax Digital works great”, “MTD for Income Tax starts 6 April”, and software recommendations.

**Pain language extracted, paraphrased**

- “I am not clear on how this works.”
- “I have both sole-trader and landlord income; does one tool handle both?”
- “Will I need two different packages?”
- “I only just scrape the threshold, so I need time to work this out.”
- “Most software feels aimed at landlords only or sole traders only.”
- “I just need a simple way to track modest income and expenses.”
- “I currently use spreadsheets and do not want over-complication.”

**Interpretation**

Reddit is the strongest source for user-language, but not necessarily a scalable acquisition channel. It validates the checker’s mixed-income branch and the “single simple solution” framing.

**Confidence:** High for pain language; Medium for demand scale.

### 6. Other social/discovery channels

**LinkedIn** was not deeply audited in this retry because the user asked mainly for Pinterest/social snippets and prior notes already establish accountant/professional SEO. However, MTD is strongly relevant to accountants, bookkeepers and tax educators. LinkedIn may work later for B2B distribution, accountant referral relationships, and “share this checker with clients” positioning.

**Facebook groups** are likely to contain landlord and sole-trader discussion, but public search access is poor and group content is often private. Do not build launch strategy around inaccessible group evidence.

**Professional newsletters/communities** may be more valuable than visual social. Accountant/bookkeeper audiences need client-ready explainers and can distribute a checker if it is neutral and source-linked.

## Viable hooks/angles

### Strongest acquisition angles

1. **“Do I need MTD?” instant clarity**
   - Best fit: landing-page hero, SEO title, Shorts/Reels hook, Pinterest checklist title.
   - Why: matches search intent and social anxiety.

2. **“Gross income, not profit”**
   - Best fit: social explainer, calculator tooltip, Reddit-style helpful answer, infographic.
   - Why: repeated confusion in existing notes and discussion evidence.

3. **“Sole trader + landlord income gets complicated”**
   - Best fit: dedicated checker branch, Reddit answer, FreeAgent-style social post, pin carousel.
   - Why: live Reddit evidence shows mixed-income software confusion.

4. **“Can I still use spreadsheets?”**
   - Best fit: software chooser, blog/FAQ, short video.
   - Why: pain point appears in Reddit and source guidance; users are wary of forced software cost.

5. **“Quarterly updates are not the same as your tax return”**
   - Best fit: reassurance copy, short clip, FAQ.
   - Why: reduces fear without making unsafe promises.

6. **“No email, HMRC login or UTR needed to check”**
   - Best fit: landing page trust bar and paid/social ad copy.
   - Why: lowers suspicion and differentiates against gated/checker funnels.

7. **“MTD software: what to check before you choose”**
   - Best fit: post-MVP comparison page and affiliate funnel.
   - Why: commercial intent exists, but trust depends on neutrality.

## MVP implications

1. **Keep MVP search-first.** The homepage should still be the checker, not a content/social hub.
2. **Design the result page for sharing.** Add copyable/plain-English result text, source links and a short “what to do next” summary. This makes it easier to share in forums, DMs or social comments without misrepresenting the tool.
3. **Create social assets from checker branches, not separate brainstorms.** Every result branch can become a pin/post/short:
   - over £50k landlord
   - £30k mixed-income user
   - below threshold now, watch later
   - spreadsheet user
   - “not sure what counts” user
4. **Use trust-first copy above the fold:** “No email needed for your result”, “Uses public HMRC guidance”, “Informational, not tax advice”, “No HMRC login required”.
5. **Add a mixed-income branch early.** Reddit evidence shows the “sole trader plus landlord” user is confused and underserved.
6. **Separate eligibility from software recommendations.** Social examples show software-led messaging is common, but user trust is fragile. Eligibility result first; software criteria and affiliate disclosure later.
7. **Make visuals simple and text-led.** MTD visuals should be checklists, thresholds, deadline timelines, decision trees and “include/exclude” cards, not generic stock imagery.
8. **Use social to warm up audiences after SEO landing pages exist.** Start with 10-15 reusable explainers after launch rather than a daily social calendar before launch.

## What not to prioritise

- **Do not prioritise Pinterest as the main acquisition engine.** Evidence is thin and the topic is not strongly visual.
- **Do not prioritise TikTok/Reels before the checker works.** Social hooks need somewhere useful to send people.
- **Do not chase vanity metrics or invent engagement.** Most platforms were partially accessible; use only visible snippets and fetched text.
- **Do not make fear-led “avoid fines” the main hook.** It may attract clicks but conflicts with the trust-first positioning already recommended.
- **Do not build a Facebook-group strategy without access.** Private group demand is plausible but not verifiable here.
- **Do not present software as “free” or “HMRC-approved” without careful caveats.** Starling’s visible Instagram comment shows users challenge pricing claims; GOV.UK says HMRC does not recommend any product or provider.
- **Do not create a broad “tax tips” social brand yet.** Keep content tightly tied to MTD eligibility, deadlines, thresholds and software-readiness.
