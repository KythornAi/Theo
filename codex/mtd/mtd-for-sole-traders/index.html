MTD for sole traders: does it apply to you, and when?

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax affects sole traders and self-employed people registered for Self Assessment whose gross self-employment income is above the relevant threshold. Here is what you need to know.

Check my MTD position → See the thresholds

What MTD for Income Tax means for sole traders

HMRC describes Making Tax Digital for Income Tax as a new way for sole traders and landlords to do Self Assessment. If you are in scope, you will need to:

  • Keep digital records of your self-employment income and expenses using compatible software.
  • Send quarterly updates to HMRC every three months. These are summaries, not tax returns.
  • Submit your final tax return through software by 31 January following the tax year, as you do now.

Source: GOV.UK: Before you use this guide

You still submit one tax return per year and pay your tax bill by 31 January. MTD adds digital record-keeping and quarterly updates on top of Self Assessment. It does not replace your annual return.

Thresholds and timeline

The income thresholds for sole traders

Qualifying income means your gross self-employment income before expenses. It is not your profit. HMRC phases the start dates by qualifying income on a specific tax year's return.

Tax return figuresQualifying incomeLikely start
2024/25More than £50,0006 April 2026
2025/26More than £30,0006 April 2027
2026/27More than £20,0006 April 2028 (announced, not yet fully legislated)

Source: GOV.UK

What counts as qualifying income for sole traders

HMRC uses your total gross income from self-employment before expenses, also called turnover. If you have more than one self-employment source, add them together.

Gross income is not profit. A sole trader with £55,000 turnover and £15,000 expenses has £40,000 profit, but their qualifying income for MTD is £55,000. The threshold uses turnover, not profit.

What does NOT count toward the threshold

  • Employment income reported through PAYE
  • Dividends, including from your own company
  • State Pension and private pensions
  • An individual partner's share of profit from a partnership
  • Limited company salary and dividends (MTD for Income Tax does not usually apply to limited companies)

Source: GOV.UK: Work out your qualifying income

Next steps for sole traders

If you are likely in the April 2026 wave

You should already be keeping digital records. Your first quarterly update is due 7 August 2026. If you have not signed up, read the GOV.UK sign-up guidance now.

If you are likely in the April 2027 wave

You have time to prepare. Check your 2025/26 figures when you file, choose compatible software, and set up a digital record-keeping routine before April 2027.

If you are not currently in scope

This could change if your gross self-employment income rises above a future threshold. Keep an eye on the £20,000 threshold from April 2028.

If you use spreadsheets now

You may be able to use bridging software that connects to your existing records. Check the GOV.UK software guidance to see what works for your setup.
