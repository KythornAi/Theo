MTD for landlords: does your rental income put you in scope?

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax affects landlords with UK or foreign property income registered for Self Assessment. The threshold uses your share of gross property income before expenses. Here is what you need to know.

Check my MTD position → Joint ownership question

Does rental income count for MTD?

Yes. HMRC says property income counts toward your qualifying income for MTD for Income Tax. The threshold uses your gross property income before expenses, meaning the rent you receive before deducting costs like mortgage interest, repairs, or agent fees.

Example from GOV.UK: £25,000 rental income plus £27,000 self-employment income equals £52,000 qualifying income. That puts you above the £50,000 threshold for the April 2026 wave.

Source: GOV.UK: Work out your qualifying income

Property income thresholds for landlords

If you are both a landlord and a sole trader, HMRC adds your gross self-employment income and your share of gross property income together.

Joint ownership, foreign property, and edge cases

Jointly owned property

Only your share of property income counts. If a jointly owned property generates £50,000 and you have an equal share, HMRC's example says your qualifying income is £25,000. If you are not sure of your share, check HMRC guidance or an adviser.

Foreign property income

If you were UK tax resident, HMRC counts UK and foreign property income on your Self Assessment return. If you were not UK resident, the rules differ. Foreign property income not declared on a UK return may not count.

Limited company landlords

If your property business runs through a limited company, MTD for Income Tax usually does not apply to that income. Companies are outside the scope of this checker.

Ceased sources

If you still have at least one continuing self-employment or property source, income from a ceased source can still be included. If all sources have ceased, you may not need MTD. Tell HMRC if this applies.

Source: GOV.UK qualifying income guidance

Software for landlords

HMRC says you need compatible software that can keep digital records of property income and expenses, send quarterly updates, and support the final tax return. Some software is designed specifically for landlords. Others are general accounting tools that cover property income.

Read the software guide for neutral criteria, or use the GOV.UK software finder to see what is listed.
