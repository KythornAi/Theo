# MTD Checker - affiliate programme research

**Last updated:** 2026-05-25

## Summary

- Best near-term fits for Kyle are **Sage**, **QuickBooks UK**, **Xero**, and **Coconut**. Sage and QuickBooks have public UK-relevant affiliate routes with clear rates or network pages; Xero has strong brand trust but hides its formal affiliate rate until approval; Coconut is the most MTD-specific but smaller and less proven.
- **FreeAgent is weak for monetisation**: I found a public referral scheme, not a cash affiliate programme. It gives subscription discounts, has strict distribution limits, and only helps if Kyle is a FreeAgent subscriber with an eligible account.
- **FreshBooks has strong affiliate economics but weak UK MTD fit**: public sources show up to $200 per paid subscriber and a 120-day cookie, but it is not positioned around UK MTD in the sources checked.
- A software-neutral checker should not rank tools purely by commission. For trust, route by user fit first: MTD relevance, UK support, landlord/sole-trader use case, pricing, and HMRC-compatible workflow. Affiliate links should be clearly disclosed.

## Scope and method

Task: research affiliate programmes for FreeAgent, Xero, QuickBooks, Sage, Coconut, and FreshBooks for UK Making Tax Digital/software referrals. For each provider I recorded commission or rate, cookie duration, network or join URL, approval requirements, restrictions, and suitability for Kyle.

Source approach:

- Built-in web search was used once to confirm QuickBooks UK FlexOffers snippets where the fetched page exposed rate but not cookie text.
- If a provider does not publish a rate or cookie duration on the accessible public page, I mark it as not public and link the best available source.

## Provider notes

### FreeAgent

Sources checked:

- FreeAgent referrals page: https://www.freeagent.com/referrals/
- FreeAgent referral support article: https://support.freeagent.com/hc/en-gb/articles/115001218770-Find-and-share-your-FreeAgent-referral-link
- FreeAgent referral scheme terms: https://www.freeagent.com/company/terms/freeagent-referral-scheme/
- FreeAgent accountant partner programme: https://www.freeagent.com/accountants/partner-programme/

Findings:

- I found a customer referral scheme and an accountant partner programme, not a public cash affiliate programme for content publishers.
- The referral page and support article both describe a 10% subscription discount for the referrer and the referred user, as long as both remain active subscribers. FreeAgent says ten successful active referrals can make the referrer's subscription free.
- If a user gets FreeAgent free via NatWest, Royal Bank of Scotland, Ulster Bank or Mettle, they can share a discount but do not receive a referrer subscription discount because their account is already free.
- Eligibility excludes accountant-provided accounts and some intermediary/reseller cases. The terms also say Apple store subscribers are not able to benefit from referral discounts.
- The referral scheme is strict about distribution: no exchange for payment, no paid publications such as sponsored posts, no voucher/discount sites, no paid advertising, no FreeAgent-like domain names, and no search keyword purchases using FreeAgent or similar terms.

Interpretation for Kyle:

- Treat FreeAgent as a product comparison option, not a revenue pillar.
- If Kyle later becomes an eligible FreeAgent subscriber, the referral link might be used as a user discount, but the checker should not depend on it for monetisation.
- Because paid publications and paid advertising are restricted, be careful if the MTD checker later runs paid traffic or sponsored content.

### Xero

Sources checked:

- Xero UK affiliate programme: https://www.xero.com/uk/affiliate-program/
- Xero refer-a-friend: https://www.xero.com/uk/campaign/refer-a-friend/
- Xero Central referral article found in search: https://central.xero.com/s/article/Refer-a-friend-to-Xero
- PartnerStack accounting directory search result: https://market.partnerstack.com/accounting

Findings:

- Xero has a formal affiliate programme for publishers, influencers and content creators. It is hosted on PartnerStack.
- Xero does not publish the exact affiliate commission or cookie duration on the public affiliate page. The page says commission depends on target audience, geography and other factors, and rates are discussed after joining.
- Xero's separate refer-a-friend page publishes a clear headline offer: 30% commission for every month a referred user remains on a paid Xero plan, up to 12 months.
- Xero says applicants must create a PartnerStack account and accept the Xero Referral Program Agreement and PartnerStack terms.
- Xero welcomes content marketing, email marketing and online advertising, but says detailed promotional terms are in the Xero Referral Program Agreement.

Interpretation for Kyle:

- Xero is worth applying to early because it is one of the strongest UK accounting brands for a search-led MTD checker.
- The checker should avoid presenting Xero as the default winner unless the result logic justifies it. Good affiliate revenue is not enough to preserve trust.
- If formal affiliate approval is slow, the public refer-a-friend route may be easier to start with, but the terms should be checked in PartnerStack before use.

### QuickBooks UK

Sources checked:

- FlexOffers Intuit QuickBooks UK active page: https://www.flexoffers.com/affiliate-programs/intuit-quickbooks-uk-affiliate-program/
- FlexOffers QuickBooks UK older/deactivated page: https://www.flexoffers.com/affiliate-programs/quickbooks-uk-affiliate-program/
- Official QuickBooks Business Affiliates page: https://quickbooks.intuit.com/partners/qbbusinessaffiliates/
- Built-in web search result for QuickBooks UK cookie/rate confirmation: https://www.flexoffers.com/affiliate-programs/intuit-quickbooks-uk-affiliate-program/

Findings:

- The live browser snapshot of the FlexOffers Intuit QuickBooks UK page shows the programme status as active and the payout as 16% of online sales and free trials, excluding QBOA Accountant purchases.
- TinyFish fetched an older QuickBooks UK FlexOffers page showing deactivated status and fixed payouts: £2.85 for free trial, £6.65 for Simple Start, £9.50 for Essentials, and £14.25 for Plus. Because that page was deactivated and another Intuit QuickBooks UK page is active, the 16% active page should be treated as the current public route.
- Web search snippet for the active FlexOffers Intuit QuickBooks UK page says the programme offers a 30-day cookie duration. The cookie term did not appear in the fetched page body, so it should be verified inside FlexOffers before launch.
- The official QuickBooks Business Affiliate page says applications are through PartnerStack and are reviewed in 7 to 10 business days, with unique referral links after approval. However, the page is US-framed, so I would not treat it as the main UK source.

Interpretation for Kyle:

- QuickBooks UK is likely a strong earning option if FlexOffers accepts the site.
- Because the official page and UK network page are not fully aligned, Kyle should use FlexOffers as the initial UK publisher route and verify exact product eligibility before going live.
- Exclude or caveat accountant purchases. The checker audience should be sole traders and landlords, not accountants buying QBOA.

### Sage

Sources checked:

- Sage UK affiliate programme: https://www.sage.com/en-gb/support/affiliate-programme/
- financeAds Sage UK page: https://www.financeads.com/programs/sage_uk/

Findings:

- Sage has a public affiliate programme page with a UK and Ireland regional option for UK, Ireland and Australia traffic, focused on small and medium businesses.
- Sage says affiliates receive Impact.com tracking links and payments through Impact.com. Applications are reviewed for quality, relevance and audience fit.
- Sage's own page publishes tiers but not exact base rates: Sage Affiliate Partner is open to approved partners, Premium requires 50 to 150 qualified actions per month, and Strategic is invitation-only at 150+ qualified actions per month.
- financeAds publishes clear Sage UK terms: cookie tracking 30 days, allowed markets UK and Ireland, validation Net30, real-time tracking, £30 CPA for online sale of Sage Accounting Cloud, £50 for a Sage Intacct lead, and £10 for sign-ups to Sage Free Accounting Software.

Interpretation for Kyle:

- Sage should be near the top of the application list. It combines UK brand trust, public CPA, 30-day cookie, and explicit MTD-related positioning on the financeAds page.
- Because Sage has both Impact.com and financeAds references, compare acceptance, terms, and payout in both networks before picking the route.
- Sage is likely the safest monetisation anchor if the checker result page is transparent and not pay-to-rank.

### Coconut

Sources checked:

- Coconut UpPromote registration page: https://af.uppromote.com/coconut-cloud-co/register
- Coconut main site: https://www.getcoconut.com/
- Coconut Times/Times+ MTD promotion terms: https://www.getcoconut.com/legal/partnership-with-the-times-and-times

Findings:

- The dynamic UpPromote page exposed the key programme fields in browser snapshot: cookie 30 days, commission type percent of sale, commission amount 10%, and an additional term saying affiliates get 10% commission on total referral sales when a customer purchases through the affiliate link or coupon code.
- The join form asks for website and social profiles, suggesting Coconut may review audience fit manually.
- Coconut's public Times/Times+ promotion terms are not affiliate terms, but they are useful context: the product being promoted is Coconut's applicable Making Tax Digital subscription plan, with standard pricing noted as £21.99 per month or £129.99 annually. The same terms say the promotional offer cannot be used with other Coconut promotional offers, discounts, referral arrangements, free trials, partner promotions or introductory pricing unless Coconut says otherwise in writing.
- I did not find public detailed affiliate restrictions such as brand bidding, PPC, coupon, or email rules before joining.

Interpretation for Kyle:

- Coconut is the most topical affiliate prospect because it is visibly marketing an MTD subscription plan to the exact audience Kyle is researching.
- Its downside is confidence: the programme is smaller, the rate is modest, and it is unclear whether 10% is recurring or only first sale. Ask/verify after applying.
- A good route would be an MTD-specialist recommendation slot, not the whole checker monetisation strategy.

### FreshBooks

Sources checked:

- FreshBooks affiliate programme: https://www.freshbooks.com/affiliate-program
- FreshBooks affiliate terms: https://www.freshbooks.com/policies/affiliate-program
- Awin FreshBooks profile: https://ui.awin.com/merchant-profile/56787

Findings:

- FreshBooks' public page says affiliates can earn up to $200 per paid subscriber and can share links in blogs, videos, social media posts, emails or other audience channels.
- Awin profile shows a 120-day attribution period and describes commissions of up to $10 per free trial plus up to $200 per paid subscriber.
- Awin says FreshBooks has migrated its affiliate programme to PartnerStack and that FreshBooks currently has full affiliate capacity, so applications may receive a slow response. It suggests emailing affiliates@freshbooks.com with the intended approach.
- FreshBooks terms allow approval or rejection at its discretion and say the Network/programme interface is the source of truth for commission structure, territory and eligible products.
- Promotional restrictions are detailed: disclose the relationship where required, avoid misleading claims, avoid unsuitable content, no brand bidding or FreshBooks terms in paid media without written approval, no direct-linking paid ads to FreshBooks with affiliate links without approval, no cookie overwriting, no coupon/deal positioning without approval, no fake codes, no expired coupons, and email marketing must comply with relevant laws.

Interpretation for Kyle:

- FreshBooks has attractive terms for a general accounting software affiliate page, but it is not the best first fit for a UK MTD checker unless compatibility and UK tax relevance are separately verified.
- It may be useful for a broader small-business-tools page later, especially if the site expands beyond MTD.

## Recommended application order

1. **Sage**: best blend of UK relevance, public rates, cookie, brand trust and MTD wording.
2. **Coconut**: most MTD-specific and likely easiest topical pitch, but verify recurrence and restrictions.
3. **QuickBooks UK**: high brand trust and public UK FlexOffers route, but verify cookie and MTD product eligibility.
4. **Xero**: high brand trust, but affiliate economics are not public until approval. Apply early due possible review delay.
5. **FreshBooks**: apply only if the checker includes a general accounting option or if FreshBooks confirms UK MTD relevance.
6. **FreeAgent**: do not prioritise for revenue unless an eligible cash affiliate programme appears or Kyle becomes an eligible subscriber and wants to share a discount.

## Implementation implications for the checker

- Build the checker as **software-neutral first**. The result page should explain why a provider appears, not just send users to the highest-paying affiliate.
- Add an affiliate disclosure near recommendations: for example, "Some links may be affiliate links. We may earn a commission if you sign up, at no extra cost to you. We still show software-neutral guidance based on your answers."
- Store provider metadata separately from eligibility logic. Commission should never drive MTD eligibility or compliance guidance.
- Before launch, verify each affiliate's accepted traffic types, PPC/brand-bidding rules, coupon rules, email rules, and whether MTD Income Tax users are an allowed target segment.

## Affiliate application framing — saved for signup

**Updated:** 2026-05-25

Kyle does **not** need to be an accountant for the publisher/affiliate routes. The accountant/bookkeeper partner programmes are separate from the affiliate programmes. For ReadyForMTD, the right framing is: publisher, comparison utility, educational checker, small-business/landlord audience.

### Main eligibility risks

Possible rejection reasons are more likely to be:

- no live website yet, or site looks unfinished
- no relevant MTD/accounting/small-business content yet
- no clear audience or traffic plan
- missing trust pages: privacy policy, affiliate disclosure, contact/about, not-tax-advice disclaimer
- site/copy sounds too official, HMRC-affiliated, or like regulated tax advice
- applying under Paper N Pixels rather than `ReadyForMTD.co.uk`, because PNP does not look tax/accounting-specific
- saying paid ads, coupon/deal traffic, or brand bidding will be used before checking whether those are allowed

### Approval-strengthening setup before applying

If possible, apply after a basic `ReadyForMTD.co.uk` holding site is live with:

- clear title: **Ready for MTD?**
- one-sentence description: “A plain-English Making Tax Digital readiness checker for UK sole traders and landlords.”
- visible disclaimer: “Not affiliated with HMRC. Not tax advice.”
- privacy policy
- affiliate disclosure
- contact email
- one starter content page, such as “Who needs Making Tax Digital for Income Tax?”

This makes the project look like a relevant publisher/comparison site rather than a random new affiliate account.

### Suggested application wording

> I run ReadyForMTD.co.uk, a UK-focused Making Tax Digital readiness checker and guidance site for sole traders and landlords. The site helps users understand whether MTD for Income Tax applies to them, when they may need to start, and what type of compatible software they may need.
>
> We are not accountants and do not provide tax advice. We link to GOV.UK/HMRC guidance, use clear affiliate disclosures, and recommend software based on user fit rather than commission alone. Traffic will come from SEO content, educational guides, and organic social/email, not brand-bidding or coupon traffic.

### Practical signup order

1. **Sage** — strongest public fit for a UK SMB/accounting audience; apply as a content publisher/comparison utility.
2. **Coconut** — best topical MTD fit; apply once the holding page is live and ask whether 10% is recurring or first-sale only.
3. **Xero** — apply as publisher/content creator, not accountant; expect approval/economics to be less transparent until inside PartnerStack.
4. **QuickBooks UK** — use the UK FlexOffers route first; verify accepted traffic types and MTD Income Tax suitability before using links.

### Build decision if affiliate approval is weak or slow

The MTD Checker can still be worth building without affiliate approvals. Affiliate links should be treated as **upside**, not the only reason to build. If approvals are pending, launch with:

- neutral software-readiness guidance
- links to GOV.UK compatible software guidance/finder
- optional paid setup checklist/template pack
- email capture for deadline reminders or setup help

Add affiliate cards later only once approval, terms, and disclosure wording are confirmed.
