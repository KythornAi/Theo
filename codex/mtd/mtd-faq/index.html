The threshold is based on gross self-employment and property income before expenses, not profit. HMRC calls this qualifying income. Yes. HMRC adds gross self-employment income and your share of gross property income together to get your qualifying income. For example, £31,000 turnover from self-employment plus £21,000 rent received equals £52,000 qualifying income.

Source: GOV.UK: Work out your qualifying income

Only your share of property income counts. HMRC's example: a jointly owned property generating £50,000 with an equal share means £25,000 qualifying income for each owner. If you are not sure of your share, check HMRC guidance or an adviser.
