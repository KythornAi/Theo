Choosing software for MTD for Income Tax

HMRC-recognised software must keep digital records, send quarterly updates, and support the final tax return. HMRC does not recommend any product or provider. Here are the neutral criteria to use.

GOV.UK software finder → What to check
Criteria first

What compatible software needs to do

HMRC says MTD for Income Tax software must allow you, or your agent, to do three things. Check these before anything else.

1

Keep digital records

Create, store, and correct digital records of self-employment and property income and expenses, by amount, date, and category.

2

Send quarterly updates

Send quarterly updates of income and expenses to HMRC every three months for each self-employment or property business.

3

Submit the tax return

Add other income sources and submit the final tax return through software by 31 January following the tax year.

Source: GOV.UK: Choose the right software

Two routes HMRC recognises

All-in-one software or bridging software

All-in-one digital record software

This creates digital records by bank feeds, receipt and invoice scanning, or manual entry. HMRC says these products usually meet all requirements and let you send quarterly updates and submit the tax return from the same tool.

Bridging software

This connects to existing records such as spreadsheets or other accounting tools. It lets you send quarterly updates and submit the tax return without re-keying everything into a new product. Good if you want to keep your current setup.

You can use more than one product, but HMRC says you can only use one product for each separate submission that needs to be made to HMRC. If using more than one, the products must work together to meet all MTD for Income Tax requirements.

Questions to ask any provider

Before you sign up, check these

  • Does it support self-employment income, property income, or both? Some products cover one but not the other.
  • Does it support all your income sources for the tax return, not just MTD ones?
  • Does it let you create new digital records, or connect to existing ones (spreadsheets)?
  • Does it support your accounting period (standard or calendar)?
  • Does it work on your devices (phone, tablet, desktop)?
  • Does it support your agent, if you use one?
  • What does it cost, and are there transaction limits on free tiers?
  • Is it listed in the GOV.UK software finder?
