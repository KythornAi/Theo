## General trends

1. **The threshold has moved.** Prompt engineering is now baseline expectation. The premium is on building production systems — reliable, measurable, integrated — not on knowing which prompt to type. This favours Theo (code-capable, system-building) over pure writers.

2. **Small businesses are the sweet spot.** Fiverr data shows SMEs are the primary buyers of automation services. They need low-code solutions (n8n, Make, Zapier) and are willing to pay for setup, not advice. Theo can deliver these without needing deep enterprise experience.

3. **Content production is always-on now.** The shift from one-off projects (a logo, a brochure) to continuous content production (Canva templates, Reels, social media calendars) is the biggest structural change on Fiverr. This aligns well with selling digital products — templates, planners, social kits — on Etsy/Gumroad.
