The 90-Second Emotional Pause

A short pause for when a strong emotion lands. Not to fight it. Not to fix it. To let the wave rise and begin to ease.

You will be guided through five gentle steps: stop, name, locate, breathe, reopen. It should take about three minutes.

Step 1 of 5

Stop.

If you can, close your eyes. If not, soften your gaze. Notice your feet on the floor, or your hands in your lap. You do not have to do anything else right now.

One slow breath in through the nose. A longer breath out. That is enough to begin.

Step 2 of 5

Name what you feel.

Silently, in your own words. A simple label is enough.

Tap a word, or write your own. There is no wrong answer.

Step 3 of 5

Where does it sit in your body?

Tap the place where you feel the feeling most clearly. The location is your own choice, the app simply records what you tap.

No location chosen yet.

Step 4 of 5

Breathe through the wave.

Slow in through the nose. A longer breath out. Let the wave rise and fall. You are not trying to push the feeling away, just giving it room to move.

Many people find the initial emotional wave peaks and begins to ease within about 90 seconds if they don't fuel it, though this varies from person to person and emotion to emotion.

The clock is here as a guide. If the wave eases sooner, end when you are ready. If it has not, stay a few breaths longer.

Step 5 of 5

Reopen.

Ask gently: what is the next wise step? It does not need to be a grand plan. The smallest good step is enough.

A drink of water. A single sentence. A two-minute walk. A message to one person. Write what comes to mind.

It is fine to leave this blank. Sometimes the wise step is just noticing.
