# Content Hooks — 2026-05-13

**Chosen Angle:** "The Swirl" — the feeling of every thought being equally loud in the ADHD brain, with no foreground/background.

**Why this angle:**
- Appearing simultaneously on Reddit AND Pinterest — shared vocabulary moment
- Highly visceral, highly relatable — every ADHD reader knows this exact feeling
- Directly maps to PNP's brain-dump → organize product flow
- Fresh metaphor — not yet saturated in ADHD content marketing
- High "that's so me" factor = high shareability / saves

---

## Variant A — Emotional Connection

**Hook:** Ever feel like every thought in your brain is screaming at once?

Not the important ones. ALL of them. Work deadlines, that email from Tuesday, what to make for dinner, a song from 2014, and "did I lock the front door?" — all equally loud, all fighting for the same microphone.

That's executive dysfunction. And no, you're not broken — your brain just doesn't know how to turn down the volume.

#ADHD #ExecutiveDysfunction #ADHDBrain #TheSwirl #Neurodivergent

## Variant B — Educational/Insight

**Hook:** Here's what executive dysfunction actually feels like.

Most people can put one thought in the foreground and keep the rest in the background. For ADHD brains? Every idea elbows for attention at the same volume. No priority. No filter. Just noise.

That paralysis you feel isn't laziness — it's your brain trying to listen to 30 conversations at once.

#ADHDAwareness #ExecutiveDysfunction #ADHDTips #Neurodiversity #BrainDump

---

## Variant C — Call to Action

**Hook:** Try this if your brain won't stop yelling at you.

1. Open a blank page. 2. Dump every single thought — no filter, no order, no judgment. 3. Walk away for 5 minutes. When you come back, your brain has space to pick what actually matters.

The "Swirl" doesn't clear itself. But it can be poured out.

Save this for your next mental logjam. 🧠✨

#ADHDHack #BrainDump #ExecutiveDysfunction #ADHDProductivity #NeurodivergentTips

## Recommendation

**Variant A has the most potential.** The visceral "screaming at the same volume" metaphor stops the scroll immediately — it makes the reader think "that's literally me." The emotional validation ("you're not broken") hits right after the pain point, which is the exact emotional arc that drives ADHD content shares and saves. Variant C is a close second for Pinterest (actionable saves), but A wins for Instagram/TikTok where emotional resonance drives reach.
