# Paper N Pixels — Pinterest & Content Strategy

> **Purpose:** Everything needed to drive organic traffic to PNP products via Pinterest.
> **The funnel:** Pinterest Pin → Blog Post / Landing Page → Lead Magnet → Email → Product
> **Rule of thumb:** Pinterest operates like Google, not social media. Users are searching, not scrolling.

---

## The Traffic Problem

Products can convert. But nobody finds them without content. PNP's biggest pre-launch risk isn't product quality — it's **discoverability**.

### The solution: Content-first Pinterest strategy

| Layer | What | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Pinterest Pins | Get discovered in search |
| 2 | Blog posts / landing pages | Warm the lead, build trust |
| 3 | Lead magnet (see `../lead-magnet/`) | Capture email |
| 4 | Email sequence | Convert to paid product |

No pin should link directly to a product page. Every pin links to **helpful content** that happens to solve what the searcher needed. The product sits at the end of that content, not at the start.

---

## Pinterest Format Best Practices

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| Aspect ratio | 2:3 (1000×1500px) — standard vertical |
| Text overlay | Large, readable font, mobile-first |
| Power words | "FREE", "TEMPLATE", "CHECKLIST", "works for ADHD brains" |
| CTA | Action-oriented: "Download your free daily reset" not "Printable planner" |
| Pin title | 100 chars max, include primary keyword |
| Pin description | 2-3 sentences, keyword-rich, tell them what they'll solve |
| Pin link | Always to blog post or landing page, never direct to product |
| Board per lead magnet | One board per freebie — don't mix with other content |
