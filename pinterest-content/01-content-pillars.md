# Content Pillars — Paper N Pixels

> Framework: Content Pillar → ADHD Pain Point → Blog Post Topic → Lead Magnet → Paid Product
> Source framework adapted from heatherfarris.com content strategy model.

---

## Pillar 1: Task Initiation & The Starting Problem

**The pain:** ADHD brains know what to do but can't start. Every planner assumes motivation is the problem. It's not. Initiation is.

**Blog topics:**
- *"Why your to-do list isn't working (and what actually helps ADHD brains start)"*
- *"The 'one thing' method: how setting one daily intention changed everything"*
- *"Task paralysis vs procrastination: knowing the difference changed how I plan"*

**Lead magnet hook:** "Free daily reset printable — one page to go from stuck to started"
**Paid upsell:** Daily Reset HTML tool (mood check + one intention + task breakdown)

---

## Pillar 2: Brain Dump & Working Memory Overload

**The pain:** Too many thoughts, no safe place to put them. Executive function burns out holding everything in working memory.

**Blog topics:**
- *"The brain dump technique for ADHD — why it works when nothing else does"*
- *"Your brain is not a filing cabinet: how to stop holding everything in working memory"*
- *"Overwhelm in 3… 2… 1: a 60-second reset for when your brain is too full"*

**Lead magnet hook:** "Free interactive brain dump tool — empty your head in 60 seconds"
**Paid upsell:** Daily Reset (full flow: brain dump → sort → plan → timer → close)

---

## Pillar 3: Time Blindness & The Missing Day

**The pain:** Hours disappear. No sense of time passing. Guilt spiral when the day is gone and nothing was done.

**Blog topics:**
- *"Time blindness isn't a character flaw — it's an ADHD brain thing (here's what helps)"*
- *"Why 'just check the time' doesn't work for ADHD brains"*
- *"The 10-minute reset: how to catch time blindness before it eats your day"*

**Lead magnet hook:** "Free daily time check-in printable — 3 quick touchpoints to stay grounded"
**Paid upsell:** Daily Reset (mood check + intention + closed-day signal)

---

## Pillar 4: Planner Abandonment & The Shame Cycle

**The pain:** Start a planner. Miss a day. Feel guilty. Abandon planner entirely. Repeat every 3 months.

**Blog topics:**
- *"Why ADHD brains abandon planners (and what actually works instead)"*
- *"The 'no streaks, no guilt' approach to daily planning"*
- *"You didn't fail the planner — the planner failed you: what ADHD-friendly tools look like"*

**Lead magnet hook:** "Free undated daily reset PDF — no streaks, no guilt, just today"
**Paid upsell:** Daily Reset (undated by design, localStorage wipes missed days)

---

## Pillar 5: Low Energy Days & Survival Mode

**The pain:** Some days are crash days. Aspirational planners add shame. Need a dignified bare-minimum mode.

**Blog topics:**
- *"What to do on an ADHD crash day (a 3-step survival plan)"*
- *"Planning for your bad days: why your system needs a low-energy mode"*
- *"The bare minimum is enough: unlearning productivity guilt"*

**Lead magnet hook:** "Free low-energy day checklist — what matters when nothing works"
**Paid upsell:** Low Energy Day Pack (£5) + Daily Reset (mood-responsive: overwhelmed path)
